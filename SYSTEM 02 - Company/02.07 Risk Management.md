## Risk Management Philosophy

Strategic risk management identifies potential threats to organizational success and implements mitigation strategies to manage them.

**Objectives**:
1. Identify risks before they become problems
2. Assess probability and impact
3. Implement mitigation strategies
4. Monitor risks continuously
5. Learn from incidents
6. Build organizational resilience

## Risk Categories

### Strategic Risks

Risks affecting organizational direction and objectives:

**Examples**:
- Market changes affecting demand
- Strategic priorities becoming misaligned
- Competitive threats
- Technology disruption
- Leadership changes

**Owner**: Managing Director

### Financial Risks

Risks affecting financial health:

**Examples**:
- Revenue shortfall
- Cost overruns
- Funding loss
- Currency fluctuations
- Investment losses

**Owner**: Managing Director (financial oversight)

### Operational Risks

Risks affecting day-to-day operations:

**Examples**:
- Process failures
- Quality failures
- Timeline delays
- Resource constraints
- Dependency failures

**Owner**: Sandy (SYSTEM 01)

### People Risks

Risks affecting team and capability:

**Examples**:
- Key person departure
- Skill gaps
- Burnout
- Conflict
- Retention challenges

**Owner**: Managing Director (strategy), Sandy (operations)

### Compliance Risks

Risks affecting regulatory or policy compliance:

**Examples**:
- Regulatory changes
- Policy violations
- Data breaches
- Legal issues
- Constitutional misalignment

**Owner**: Managing Director

### Technical Risks

Risks affecting systems and technology:

**Examples**:
- System failures
- Security breaches
- Technology obsolescence
- Integration failures
- Data loss

**Owner**: Sandy / Specialists

## Risk Management Process

### 1. Identify Risks

**Methods**:
- Regular brainstorming
- Historical analysis
- Expert assessment
- Stakeholder input
- Trend monitoring
- Scenario planning

**Ownership**: Continuous responsibility across organization

### 2. Assess Risks

For each identified risk:

**Probability Assessment**: How likely is this?
- High (>50%)
- Medium (20-50%)
- Low (<20%)

**Impact Assessment**: If it happens, what's the impact?
- High (threatens objectives or viability)
- Medium (significant impact but manageable)
- Low (minor impact)

**Risk Prioritization**: Priority = Probability × Impact

### 3. Plan Mitigation

For significant risks:

**Mitigation Strategies**:
- **Avoid**: Eliminate the risk (e.g., don't do the risky thing)
- **Reduce**: Lower probability or impact (most common)
- **Accept**: Accept the risk if mitigation cost exceeds benefit
- **Transfer**: Transfer risk to another party (e.g., insurance)

**Mitigation Plan**:
- What will be done
- Who is responsible
- Timeline
- Resources needed
- Expected outcome

### 4. Monitor Risks

**Ongoing Monitoring**:
- Is the risk still valid?
- Is probability or impact changing?
- Is mitigation working?
- Are new risks emerging?

**Frequency**: Monthly operational risks; Quarterly strategic risks

### 5. Learn and Improve

When risks materialize:

- Understand what happened
- Assess why mitigation didn't work
- Implement improvements
- Update risk management
- Share learning

## Risk Register

A centralized risk register tracks:

**For Each Risk**:
- Risk description
- Category
- Probability and impact
- Mitigation strategy
- Owner
- Status
- Last updated
- Next review date

**Management**:
- Updated at least monthly
- Reviewed in leadership meetings
- Accessible to relevant stakeholders

## Risk Tolerance

Organization has different risk tolerances:

**Strategic Risks**: Lower tolerance; only after careful analysis

**Innovation Risks**: Higher tolerance; managed experimentation encouraged

**Operational Risks**: Low tolerance; prevented through process

**Financial Risks**: Low tolerance; carefully managed

**Compliance Risks**: Very low tolerance; avoided

This is communicated so decisions align with risk tolerance.

## Crisis Readiness

Beyond individual risk management:

**Preparation**:
- Crisis management plan (see SYSTEM 01)
- Communication protocols
- Decision authorities
- Resource pre-planning
- Scenario training

**Response Capability**:
- Crisis team identified
- Communication channels clear
- Authorities understood
- Resources available

## Risk Communication

Risks are communicated:

**To Leadership**: Strategic and significant risks monthly

**To Team**: Risks affecting their area

**To Stakeholders**: Material risks as needed

**Transparency**: Risk status is visible; culture normalizes risk discussion

## Insurance and Risk Transfer

Where appropriate, risks are transferred through insurance:

**Coverage Consideration**:
- Professional liability
- Directors and officers liability
- Cyber liability
- Property and casualty
- Employment practices

**Decision**: Insurance decisions are made strategically based on risk profile

## Business Continuity

The organization can continue if challenges occur:

**Considerations**:
- Key function redundancy
- Knowledge preservation
- Critical process documentation
- Crisis communication
- Recovery procedures

**Testing**: Annually tested; procedures updated based on testing
