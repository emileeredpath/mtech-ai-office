## Governance Framework

Governance defines how decisions are made and who has authority for what.

### Governance Principles

1. **Clarity**: Who decides what is explicit
2. **Accountability**: Decisions have clear owners
3. **Transparency**: Decision-making is visible
4. **Authority Alignment**: Authority matches responsibility
5. **Escalation**: Clear paths for issues exceeding authority
6. **Review**: Governance is regularly reviewed
7. **Balance**: Efficiency and oversight are balanced

### Decision Authority

**Strategic Decisions** (Managing Director):
- Direction and strategy
- Major investments
- Organizational changes
- Constitutional questions
- External commitments

**Operational Decisions** (Sandy via SYSTEM 01):
- Work prioritization
- Process changes
- Resource allocation
- Project decisions
- Quality standards

**Execution Decisions** (Specialists):
- How work is done
- Specific approach
- Technical decisions
- Innovation within domain

## Board/Leadership Oversight

**Oversight Responsibilities**:
- Strategic direction approval
- Performance monitoring
- Risk oversight
- Financial oversight
- Constitution compliance
- Major decisions

**Review Frequency**:
- Monthly: Financial and operational metrics
- Quarterly: Strategic progress and performance
- Annually: Comprehensive review and planning

**Escalation to Leadership**:
Strategic issues, significant risks, major decisions, Constitutional questions

## Compliance Requirements

### Constitutional Compliance

Every decision must be assessed for alignment with SYSTEM 00 - Constitution.

**Review Process**:
- Strategic decisions are reviewed for Constitutional alignment
- When uncertainty exists, escalate for guidance
- Constitution always takes precedence

### Internal Policy Compliance

Organizational policies are developed and followed:

**Policy Areas**:
- Quality standards
- Communication standards
- Project management
- Resource allocation
- Ethical standards

**Enforcement**:
- Sandy monitors compliance
- Non-compliance is addressed
- Regular audit of compliance
- Updates as policies evolve

### External Compliance

Compliance with external requirements:

**Regulatory**:
- Industry regulations
- Legal requirements
- Data protection
- Privacy laws
- Employment laws

**Contractual**:
- Client agreements
- Partner agreements
- Vendor agreements
- Loan covenants

**Best Practice**:
- Industry standards
- Best practice frameworks
- Certification requirements

### Compliance Monitoring

**Responsibility**: Managing Director (ultimate), Sandy (operational)

**Methods**:
- Regular audits
- Policy reviews
- Incident reporting
- Feedback from specialists
- Compliance assessments

**Frequency**:
- Monthly: Operational compliance
- Quarterly: Strategic and regulatory compliance
- Annually: Comprehensive compliance review

## Risk Governance

**Strategic Risk**: Managed by Managing Director and Board

**Operational Risk**: Managed by Sandy

**Specialized Risk**: Managed by system owners

**Escalation**: Significant risks escalate to leadership

See 02.08 Risk Management for detailed framework.

## Conflict of Interest

Conflicts of interest are avoided and disclosed:

**Policy**:
- Decisions are made in organization's interest
- Personal interests don't influence decisions
- Conflicts are disclosed promptly
- Mitigation strategies are implemented

**Examples**:
- Personal financial interest in vendor
- Relationship conflicts
- Competing organizational interests
- External role conflicts

## Financial Governance

**Responsibility**: Managing Director with finance oversight

**Controls**:
- Budget approval process
- Spending authorization levels
- Regular financial reporting
- Audit procedures
- Investment decisions

**Oversight**:
- Monthly financial reporting
- Quarterly review
- Annual audit
- Strategic decisions

## Data Governance

**Responsibility**: Managing Director (strategic), Sandy (operational)

**Principles**:
- Data security
- Privacy protection
- Data accuracy
- Appropriate use
- Access control

**Management**:
- Data inventory
- Security protocols
- Privacy compliance
- Breach response

## Decision Documentation

All major decisions are documented:

**Documentation Includes**:
- What decision was made
- When it was made
- Who made it
- Why it was made
- Authority used
- Rationale

**Storage**: Centralized decision log

**Accessibility**: Available to those who need context

## Change Management

Significant changes go through governance process:

**Changes Requiring Approval**:
- Strategy changes
- Structure changes
- Major policy changes
- Governance changes

**Process**:
1. Clear problem/opportunity identification
2. Options developed
3. Analysis completed
4. Recommendation prepared
5. Leadership approval
6. Communication
7. Implementation
8. Monitoring

## Annual Governance Review

Annually, governance is reviewed:

**Assessment**:
- Is governance working?
- Are decisions being made appropriately?
- Are escalations working?
- Is compliance being maintained?
- Do any changes need to be made?

**Updates**: Governance is adjusted based on review

## Communication of Governance

Governance is communicated:

**To Team**: Clear understanding of how decisions are made

**To Leadership**: Accountability structures are understood

**To Stakeholders**: Who can make what decisions

Clarity reduces confusion and ensures smooth operation.
