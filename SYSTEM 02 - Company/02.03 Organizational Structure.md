## Organizational Design Principles

The organization is structured to:

1. **Enable Specialization**: Experts focus on their domain
2. **Clarify Accountability**: Responsibilities are clear
3. **Enable Coordination**: Sandy (SYSTEM 01) coordinates efforts
4. **Reduce Overhead**: Minimal bureaucracy
5. **Scale Smoothly**: Structure scales with growth
6. **Preserve Culture**: Structure reflects values
7. **Support Strategy**: Structure enables strategic execution

## Current Organizational Structure

```
Managing Director
├─ SYSTEM 01 - Sandy (Operations)
│  └─ Specialist Coordination
│
└─ Leadership Functions
   ├─ Strategic Planning
   ├─ External Relations
   ├─ Financial Management
   └─ Governance Oversight
```

## Role Definitions

### Managing Director

**Accountable For**:
- Strategic direction and decisions
- Organizational performance
- Constitutional compliance
- Leadership oversight
- External representation
- Board/Investor relations

**Works With**:
- Sandy (operational execution)
- Specialists (expertise and delivery)
- External stakeholders

**Does Not**:
- Execute operational details
- Manage day-to-day activities
- Directly coordinate specialists

### Sandy (SYSTEM 01)

**Accountable For**:
- Operational coordination
- Work prioritization
- Project management
- Quality assurance
- Knowledge preservation
- Team coordination
- Risk management
- Process improvement

**Reports To**: Managing Director

**Works With**:
- Specialists (delegation, coordination)
- Managing Director (strategy implementation)
- SYSTEM 02-08 (specialized functions)

### Specialists

**Accountable For**:
- Their domain expertise
- Quality of their work
- Meeting assigned deadlines
- Following standards
- Continuous improvement
- Knowledge sharing

**Report To**: Sandy (work coordination) / Managing Director (performance)

**Work With**:
- Other specialists (collaboration)
- Sandy (coordination)
- External stakeholders (delivery)

## Organizational Layers

### Strategic Layer

**Role**: Set direction, make major decisions

**Functions**:
- Strategic planning
- Market positioning
- Major investment decisions
- External relationships

**Leadership**: Managing Director

### Operational Layer

**Role**: Coordinate execution, maintain quality

**Functions**:
- Work prioritization
- Project management
- Quality assurance
- Knowledge management
- Risk management

**Leadership**: Sandy (SYSTEM 01)

### Specialized Layer

**Role**: Provide expertise, execute work

**Functions**:
- Domain expertise
- Quality execution
- Innovation within domain
- Knowledge contribution

**Leadership**: Specialist domain owners

## Coordination Across Layers

**Strategic → Operational**: Strategy is translated into priorities and projects

**Operational → Specialists**: Projects are broken into tasks and assigned

**Specialists → Operational**: Work is completed; progress is reported

**Operational → Strategic**: Intelligence informs strategy adjustments

## Span of Control

### Managing Director

Direct reports: Few (strategic focus)

Oversight: All organizational functions through accountability structures

### Sandy

Direct coordination: All active projects and work

Team size: Scales with work volume; typically 5-30 people

### Specialists

Focus: Their domain; coordinate with other specialists

Reporting relationships: To Sandy (operational) and Managing Director (performance)

## Growth and Structure Evolution

As organization grows:

**Stage 1 (Current)**: Flat; Managing Director → Sandy → Specialists

**Stage 2 (5-10 people)**: Emerging specialization; clusters may form

**Stage 3 (10-30 people)**: Multi-team structure; team leads may emerge

**Stage 4 (30+ people)**: Departmental structure; layer of management added

Structure evolution principles:
- Maintain clear accountability
- Preserve culture
- Avoid bureaucracy
- Keep communication flowing
- Clarify reporting relationships

## Decision-Making Structure

**Strategic Decisions**: Managing Director with input from Sandy and relevant specialists

**Operational Decisions**: Sandy with consultation

**Execution Decisions**: Specialists within their domain

**Boundary Decisions** (between layers): Collaborative decision-making

Escalation path is clear at each level.

## Communication Structure

**Vertical**: Between layers for direction and reporting

**Horizontal**: Between specialists for coordination

**Cross-Functional**: Across domains for complex projects

Communication channels:
- Hierarchical (up/down reporting)
- Peer-to-peer (between specialists)
- Cross-functional (project teams)
- All-hands (organization-wide)

## Organizational Flexibility

The structure is designed to be flexible:

- Temporary teams for specific projects
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Specialist roles may overlap
- Responsibilities can shift as needs change

Flexibility is balanced with:
- Clear current role definitions
- Clear accountability
- Transparent changes
- Change management
