## Performance Management Philosophy

Performance management aligns organizational activities with strategic objectives and measures progress systematically.

**Objectives**:
1. Clarify expectations
2. Measure performance
3. Provide feedback
4. Enable improvement
5. Recognize achievement
6. Make data-driven decisions

## Performance Metrics Framework

### Strategic Metrics

Measure progress toward strategic objectives:

**Examples**:
- Revenue growth
- Market share
- Strategic objective achievement
- Capability development
- Customer acquisition

**Frequency**: Quarterly review

### Operational Metrics

Measure operational effectiveness:

**Examples**:
- Project completion rate
- Quality metrics
- Schedule performance
- Resource utilization
- Efficiency metrics

**Frequency**: Monthly review

### Financial Metrics

Measure financial health:

**Examples**:
- Revenue
- Profitability
- Cost per outcome
- Cash flow
- Return on investment

**Frequency**: Monthly review; quarterly analysis

### Team Metrics

Measure team health:

**Examples**:
- Engagement scores
- Retention rates
- Development progress
- Satisfaction scores
- Capability growth

**Frequency**: Quarterly pulse checks; annual surveys

## Performance Measurement

**Approach**:
- Metrics are clearly defined
- Data collection is systematic
- Measurement is objective
- Trends are analyzed
- Comparisons are made

**Reporting**:
- Monthly dashboards
- Quarterly reviews
- Annual assessments
- Trend analysis
- Variance explanation

## Performance Reviews

### Monthly Reviews

**Focus**: Operational performance

**Activities**:
- Metrics review
- Progress toward objectives
- Issue identification
- Corrective actions

**Participants**: Leadership, Sandy

### Quarterly Reviews

**Focus**: Strategic progress and performance

**Activities**:
- Strategic objective progress
- Overall performance
- Trends and patterns
- Adjustments needed

**Participants**: Leadership, key stakeholders

### Annual Reviews

**Focus**: Year in review and planning for next year

**Activities**:
- Full-year performance
- Strategic achievement
- Learning and improvement
- Next-year planning

**Participants**: Leadership, all stakeholders

## Performance Standards

**Clarity**: Standards are explicit and communicated

**Achievability**: Standards are realistic but challenging

**Measurement**: Standards are measurable

**Communication**: Everyone understands standards

**Review**: Standards are reviewed annually

## Performance Feedback

Feedback is provided:

**Timing**: Regular and ongoing

**Format**:
- Formal (quarterly/annual reviews)
- Informal (regular conversations)
- Written (performance reports)
- Verbal (discussions)

**Quality**:
- Specific and observable
- Constructive and respectful
- Actionable
- Timely

## Performance Recognition

Achievement is recognized:

**Recognition Types**:
- Formal recognition in meetings
- Compensation adjustments
- Development opportunities
- Special assignments
- Public acknowledgment

**Criteria**: Recognition is based on performance against standards

## Performance Improvement

When performance falls short:

**Process**:
1. Clear assessment of gap
2. Discussion with performer
3. Understanding of root cause
4. Improvement plan development
5. Support and resources
6. Regular monitoring
7. Assessment of improvement

**Support**: Training, coaching, mentoring as needed

## Performance and Compensation

Compensation reflects performance:

**Principles**:
- Market alignment
- Performance-based
- Fair and transparent
- Consistent application
- Regular review

**Review Frequency**: Annually minimum

## Organizational Performance

Organization's overall performance is assessed:

**Dimensions**:
- Strategic objective achievement
- Financial performance
- Operational effectiveness
- Team engagement
- Quality and compliance
- Growth and scaling
- Risk management

**Assessment**: Quarterly and annual

## Performance Data Use

Performance data informs:

- Strategic decisions
- Resource allocation
- Process improvements
- Training needs
- Organizational changes
- Individual development
- Compensation decisions

Data is used constructively to improve performance.
