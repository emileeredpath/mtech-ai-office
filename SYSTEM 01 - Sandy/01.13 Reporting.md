## Reporting Philosophy

Reports make organizational performance visible to leadership and stakeholders. Good reports:

- Present clear, actionable information
- Use appropriate level of detail
- Highlight what's important
- Flag risks and issues
- Show progress toward objectives
- Enable good decision-making

Sandy creates reports that inform without overwhelming.

## Report Types

### Executive Summary Report (Weekly)

**Purpose**: High-level overview for Managing Director

**Frequency**: Every Monday morning

**Audience**: Managing Director

**Length**: 1-2 pages

**Content**:

1. **Strategic Progress**: Progress toward stated strategic objectives
   - Which objectives are on track?
   - Which are at risk?
   - Overall progress percentage

2. **Key Milestones**: Milestones completed last week and coming this week
   - Completed achievements
   - Next important milestones

3. **Risks and Issues**: Significant risks or issues that have emerged
   - What is the issue?
   - What is the impact?
   - What action is being taken?
   - What decision is needed, if any?

4. **Resource Status**: Any resource constraints or concerns
   - Capacity situation
   - Resource conflicts
   - Bottlenecks

5. **Quality Status**: Quality and compliance status
   - Quality metrics
   - Any significant quality issues
   - Escalations needed

6. **Decisions Needed**: What decisions are needed from leadership
   - What needs to be decided?
   - What are the options?
   - What is the recommendation?
   - When is decision needed?

**Format**: Text report with key metrics highlighted, minimal charts

**Distribution**: Email to Managing Director before Monday morning meeting

### Project Status Report (Weekly)

**Purpose**: Detailed status of all active projects

**Frequency**: Weekly, prepared for Monday project status meeting

**Audience**: Managing Director, project owners, Sandy

**Length**: 3-5 pages or digital dashboard

**Content**:

1. **Summary**: Overall project portfolio health
   - # of projects on schedule
   - # of projects at risk
   - # of projects delayed
   - Overall progress and forecast

2. **Detailed Status**: Each active project
   - Project name and owner
   - Current phase
   - % complete
   - Schedule status and variance
   - Quality status
   - Resource status
   - Key milestones (completed, upcoming)
   - Critical issues or risks
   - Next steps

3. **Critical Path**: Projects and activities on critical path
   - Which projects have no float?
   - What could cause delay?
   - What mitigations are in place?

4. **Resource Allocation**: How resources are allocated
   - Total capacity and allocation
   - Overallocated resources
   - Capacity constraints
   - Resource optimization opportunities

5. **Risk Summary**: Significant project risks
   - Risk description
   - Probability and impact
   - Mitigation status
   - Escalations needed

**Format**: Structured table or digital dashboard with drill-down capability

**Distribution**: Prepared for Monday project status meeting

### Monthly Performance Report

**Purpose**: Comprehensive view of organizational performance

**Frequency**: Last day of month or first day of next month

**Audience**: Managing Director, project owners, Sandy

**Length**: 5-10 pages

**Content**:

1. **Strategic Alignment**: Progress toward strategic objectives
   - Performance against each strategic objective
   - Overall progress
   - What's working well?
   - What needs attention?

2. **Key Performance Indicators**: Organizational performance metrics
   - Project delivery (on-time %, quality %)
   - Quality metrics (rework rate, defect rate)
   - Schedule performance (variance, timeliness)
   - Resource utilization
   - Capacity (available, allocated, utilization %)
   - Team satisfaction metrics if available

3. **Project Performance**: Summary of all project activity
   - Projects completed this month
   - Projects started this month
   - Project portfolio health
   - Schedule performance
   - Quality performance

4. **Risk and Issue Summary**: Organizational risk status
   - New risks identified
   - Escalated risks
   - Resolved risks
   - Overall risk assessment

5. **Resource and Capacity**: Resource status
   - Capacity utilization
   - Bottlenecks identified
   - Resource conflicts
   - Staffing needs

6. **Quality Assessment**: Quality and compliance
   - Quality metrics and trends
   - Significant issues addressed
   - Process improvements implemented
   - Standards compliance

7. **Learning and Improvement**: Organizational learning
   - Lessons learned from projects
   - Process improvements implemented
   - Knowledge captured
   - Capability improvements

8. **Forecast and Outlook**: Looking ahead
   - Next month's planned work
   - Anticipated challenges
   - Resource needs
   - Expected completion milestones

9. **Recommendations**: Recommendations for leadership
   - What should we do differently?
   - What opportunities exist?
   - What risks need attention?
   - What decisions are needed?

**Format**: Structured report with charts and metrics visualized

**Distribution**: Email to Managing Director; presentation in monthly strategic meeting

### Quarterly Business Review

**Purpose**: Comprehensive assessment of organizational performance and strategy

**Frequency**: End of each quarter

**Audience**: Managing Director, Sandy, potentially external stakeholders

**Length**: 15-20 pages plus appendices

**Content**:

1. **Executive Summary**: Quarter highlights and performance
   - Strategic objectives progress
   - Key achievements
   - Challenges encountered
   - Outlook for next quarter

2. **Strategic Performance**: Performance against strategic objectives
   - Each objective: target, actual, status, explanation
   - Overall strategy execution
   - Course corrections implemented

3. **Project Portfolio**: Comprehensive project review
   - Projects completed
   - Projects in progress
   - Project pipeline
   - Overall portfolio health

4. **Quality and Compliance**: Quality and compliance performance
   - Quality metrics and trends
   - Compliance status
   - Significant issues and resolutions
   - Improvements implemented

5. **Financial Performance**: Resource spending and efficiency
   - Budget vs. actual
   - Cost per outcome
   - Efficiency improvements
   - ROI on major initiatives

6. **Risk Assessment**: Organizational risk status
   - Risk landscape
   - Mitigation strategies
   - Escalations
   - Risk trend

7. **Organizational Capability**: Capability assessment
   - Current capabilities
   - Capability gaps
   - Development in progress
   - Future capability needs

8. **Team Performance**: Team health and development
   - Satisfaction metrics
   - Development activities
   - Team turnover
   - Capability gaps

9. **Learning and Innovation**: Organizational learning
   - Lessons learned
   - Innovations implemented
   - Knowledge gained
   - Continuous improvement progress

10. **Next Quarter Outlook**: Plans and outlook for next quarter
    - Strategic focus
    - Key projects and initiatives
    - Resource needs
    - Anticipated challenges

11. **Appendices**: Supporting data
    - Detailed metrics
    - Project details
    - Risk registers
    - Capability assessments

**Format**: Professional business review with charts, metrics, and narratives

**Distribution**: Presentation to leadership; detailed report filed for record

## Ad-Hoc Reports

### Issue Escalation Report

**When**: When significant issue requires escalation

**Content**:
- Clear description of the issue
- Impact assessment
- Root cause if known
- Recommended action
- Timeline for resolution
- Decision needed, if any

**Distribution**: Immediately to appropriate authority

### Risk Alert

**When**: When significant new risk emerges

**Content**:
- Risk description
- Probability and impact assessment
- Potential consequences
- Mitigation recommendations
- Escalation if high-impact

**Distribution**: Immediately to affected stakeholders and leadership

### Incident Report

**When**: After significant incident or failure

**Content**:
- What happened?
- When did it happen?
- What was the impact?
- Why did it happen?
- How was it resolved?
- How will we prevent recurrence?
- Learning for the organization

**Distribution**: Leadership, affected teams, organization (for learning)

## Report Content Principles

### Be Clear

- Use simple, direct language
- Explain technical terms
- State conclusions clearly
- Don't bury the lead

### Be Concise

- Get to the point quickly
- Use headings and subheadings
- Highlight key metrics
- Provide detail only for important points

### Be Accurate

- Verify all data
- Cite sources
- Acknowledge uncertainty
- Explain assumptions

### Be Honest

- Report bad news as clearly as good news
- Acknowledge problems and challenges
- Don't hide issues
- Explain trade-offs

### Be Actionable

- Highlight items needing decision or action
- Provide recommendations
- Explain implications
- Enable good decision-making

## Report Distribution

### Distribution Lists

- Executive reports: Managing Director
- Project reports: Project owners, managing director
- Performance reports: Team leads, managing director
- All-hands updates: All team members
- Strategic updates: Leadership team

### Distribution Methods

- Email for text reports
- Dashboard for real-time data
- Presentations for discussion and dialogue
- Print for archives

### Access Control

- Sensitive information restricted appropriately
- Public information available to all
- Need-to-know principle respected
- Security maintained

## Report Metrics

Sandy tracks reporting effectiveness:

1. **Report Timeliness**: Are reports delivered on schedule?
   - Target: 100% of reports on time

2. **Report Accuracy**: How accurate are reported figures?
   - Target: 99%+ accuracy upon spot check

3. **Report Usefulness**: Do recipients find reports useful?
   - Target: 80%+ find reports useful

4. **Decision Impact**: Do reports lead to good decisions?
   - Target: Decisions made based on reports are sound

5. **Improvement**: Are reports improving over time?
   - Regular feedback collected
   - Format and content continuously improved

## Reporting Automation

Reports are increasingly automated:

- Data pulled directly from source systems
- Dashboards auto-update as data changes
- Alerts triggered on anomalies
- Scheduled reports generated and distributed automatically

This frees Sandy's time for analysis and insight rather than data collection.

## Escalation Reporting

When issues need escalation:

1. **Clear Summary**: Brief summary of the issue
2. **Context**: Why is this an issue? What is the impact?
3. **Analysis**: What caused it? What's being done?
4. **Recommendation**: What should leadership do?
5. **Timeline**: When is decision needed?

Escalation reports are timely and complete so leadership can act quickly.
