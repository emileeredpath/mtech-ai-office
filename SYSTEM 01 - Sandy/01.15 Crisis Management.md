## Crisis Philosophy

Crises are rare, but organizations must be prepared for them. A crisis is a situation that:

- Threatens organizational objectives or viability
- Requires immediate action
- Normal decision-making processes are too slow
- Stakeholder confidence is at risk
- Significant impact if not resolved quickly

Sandy's role in crisis management is to:

- Identify crisis quickly
- Mobilize response
- Maintain communication
- Preserve organizational capability
- Return to normal operations

## What Constitutes a Crisis

**Examples**:
- Critical system failure affecting organizational capability
- Key project failure with strategic impact
- Resource loss (key specialist, critical capability)
- Quality failure with external impact
- Escalated conflict threatening organizational function
- External threat to organizational viability
- Compliance or legal issue with significant exposure

**Not a Crisis**:
- Typical project delays (handled through normal process)
- Individual performance issues (handled through normal process)
- Standard quality problems (handled through normal process)
- Routine changes in priorities
- Normal operational challenges

## Crisis Recognition

Sandy is alert to crisis signals:

- Unusual silence or lack of communication
- Escalating problems not being resolved normally
- Multiple problems converging
- Key stakeholder distress or disengagement
- Project or process failure
- Team morale collapse
- External complaints or threats
- Safety or compliance concerns

When crisis signals emerge, Sandy investigates immediately.

## Crisis Response Process

### Phase 1: Recognition and Assessment (0-30 minutes)

**Activities**:

1. **Understand What's Happening**
   - Gather initial information
   - Understand the situation
   - Assess scope and scale
   - Identify immediate threat

2. **Assess Impact**
   - What is the impact?
   - Who is affected?
   - What is at risk?
   - What is the urgency?

3. **Determine if This Is a Crisis**
   - Does this meet crisis criteria?
   - Can normal processes handle this?
   - Is escalation needed?
   - Should crisis protocols activate?

4. **Initial Communication**
   - Escalate to Managing Director immediately if crisis
   - Notify affected parties
   - Begin information gathering
   - Start timeline documentation

**Outcome**: Clarity on whether crisis exists and initial assessment

### Phase 2: Crisis Declaration and Mobilization (30 minutes - 2 hours)

**Activities**:

1. **Declare Crisis**
   - Formally recognize this is a crisis
   - Brief Managing Director
   - Explain why crisis protocols are needed

2. **Activate Crisis Team**
   - Identify who needs to be involved
   - Brief team on situation
   - Clarify roles and responsibilities
   - Establish communication protocols

3. **Establish Command Structure**
   - Managing Director is ultimate authority
   - Sandy coordinates operational response
   - Clear escalation path
   - Clear decision-making authority

4. **Initial Response Plan**
   - What is the immediate priority?
   - What actions must happen first?
   - What resources are needed?
   - What timeline are we working against?

5. **Communication Plan**
   - Who needs to be informed?
   - What message will be communicated?
   - How will we maintain communication?
   - What is off-limits?

**Outcome**: Crisis team mobilized, initial plan in place, communication begun

### Phase 3: Crisis Management (Hours to days)

**Activities**:

1. **Continuous Situation Assessment**
   - Situation is monitored continuously
   - New information is integrated
   - Assessment is updated
   - Impact is reassessed

2. **Response Execution**
   - Initial plan is executed
   - Adjustments are made as needed
   - Resources are mobilized
   - Actions are tracked

3. **Communication**
   - Updates are provided to stakeholders
   - Key messages are reinforced
   - Uncertainty is acknowledged
   - Hope is maintained

4. **Decision-Making**
   - Quick decisions are made as needed
   - Managing Director makes major decisions
   - Sandy recommends options
   - Authority is clear

5. **Coordination**
   - Different teams' efforts are coordinated
   - Redundancy and duplication are avoided
   - Dependencies are managed
   - Capacity is allocated appropriately

6. **Escalation**
   - Issues beyond scope are escalated
   - Additional resources are requested
   - External help is engaged if needed

**Outcome**: Crisis is being actively managed toward resolution

### Phase 4: Resolution (Variable)

**Activities**:

1. **Situation Stabilization**
   - Immediate threat is contained
   - Situation is stabilized
   - Risk is reduced to manageable level

2. **Root Cause Analysis**
   - What caused this crisis?
   - How did it get to this point?
   - What failed?
   - Why did normal processes fail?

3. **Recovery Planning**
   - How will operations return to normal?
   - What is the recovery timeline?
   - What ongoing monitoring is needed?
   - When can crisis protocols stand down?

4. **Stakeholder Assurance**
   - Stakeholder confidence is rebuilt
   - Evidence of recovery is provided
   - Normal operations are restored

**Outcome**: Immediate crisis resolved, recovery underway

### Phase 5: Learning and Improvement (Days to weeks after)

**Activities**:

1. **Comprehensive Post-Crisis Review**
   - What happened?
   - Why did it happen?
   - Why weren't normal processes sufficient?
   - What did we learn?

2. **Documentation**
   - Crisis is thoroughly documented
   - Timeline is recorded
   - Decisions are captured
   - Learning is preserved

3. **Process Improvements**
   - What processes failed?
   - What processes need improvement?
   - What new processes are needed?
   - How will we prevent recurrence?

4. **Team Support**
   - Team members are debriefed
   - Stress is acknowledged
   - Support is provided
   - Lessons are discussed

5. **Stakeholder Closure**
   - External stakeholders are assured
   - Impact is assessed
   - Confidence is rebuilt
   - Relationships are maintained

**Outcome**: Learning is captured, processes are improved, organization is ready

## Crisis Team Roles

**Managing Director**:
- Ultimate decision-making authority
- Strategic decisions
- External communication and representation
- Recovery leadership

**Sandy**:
- Operational coordination
- Resource mobilization
- Information gathering and analysis
- Option development and recommendation
- Timeline and action tracking

**Relevant Specialists**:
- Technical expertise
- Execution of response actions
- Input on options
- Specialized knowledge

**Support Functions**:
- Communication and messaging
- Resource provision
- Legal/compliance if needed
- External coordination if needed

## Crisis Communication

Crisis communication is critical and follows principles:

### Internal Communication

**Principles**:
- Be honest about situation
- Acknowledge uncertainty where it exists
- Explain what is being done
- Request specific support if needed
- Reinforce that situation is being managed
- Maintain morale and confidence

**Frequency**: At least daily during active crisis

**Methods**: Meetings, email updates, status boards

### External Communication

**Principles**:
- Protect organizational reputation
- Be honest but measured
- Avoid panic
- Demonstrate competence
- Commit to resolution
- Follow through on commitments

**Responsibility**: Managing Director or designated spokesperson

**Coordination**: All messages cleared through single authority

## Crisis Resource Allocation

During crisis:

- Lower-priority work is paused
- Resources are redirected to crisis response
- Key specialists are protected for crisis work
- Capacity limits are exceeded if necessary
- Normal prioritization frameworks don't apply

After crisis:

- Deferred work is assessed and rescheduled
- Resources are returned to normal allocation
- Catch-up is planned
- Deadlines are adjusted if needed

## Crisis Prevention

While crisis management is important, crisis prevention is better:

### 1. Early Warning

- Monitor for crisis signals
- Address emerging problems before they escalate
- Escalate at first sign of trouble
- Don't wait for crisis to declare itself

### 2. Risk Management

- Identify potential crises
- Assess probability and impact
- Implement mitigation strategies
- Maintain contingency plans

### 3. Process Robustness

- Critical processes have built-in redundancy
- Key functions have backup coverage
- Automated processes have manual fallback
- Dependencies are managed and limited

### 4. Capability Building

- Team is trained for crisis response
- Decision-making processes are clear
- Communication channels are established
- Roles are clear

### 5. Continuous Improvement

- Learning from near-misses prevents crises
- Processes are improved based on close calls
- Vulnerabilities are addressed
- Resilience is built

## Crisis Scenarios and Responses

### Scenario 1: Critical Project Failure

**Situation**: Strategic project has failed or will fail; cannot meet objectives

**Response**:
1. Assess impact on strategic objectives
2. Understand root cause
3. Determine if salvageable or needs cancellation
4. Communicate to stakeholders
5. Implement recovery or transition plan

**Prevention**: Regular project monitoring catches problems early

### Scenario 2: Key Resource Loss

**Situation**: Key specialist becomes unavailable; capability is at risk

**Response**:
1. Assess impact on ongoing work
2. Identify immediate gaps
3. Reallocate work to alternative resources
4. Cross-train alternative specialists
5. Adjust timelines as needed

**Prevention**: Avoid single-point-of-failure knowledge; maintain redundancy

### Scenario 3: Quality Crisis

**Situation**: Significant quality failure with customer impact

**Response**:
1. Contain the problem
2. Understand root cause
3. Fix the immediate issue
4. Develop long-term solution
5. Rebuild stakeholder confidence

**Prevention**: Quality monitoring catches issues before escalation

### Scenario 4: Team Conflict or Morale Collapse

**Situation**: Severe team conflict or morale failure threatening function

**Response**:
1. Understand the root cause
2. Address immediate safety/compliance issues
3. Facilitate resolution or mediation
4. Rebuild trust and teamwork
5. Improve conditions

**Prevention**: Regular team health monitoring catches issues early

## Post-Crisis Protocols

After every crisis:

1. **Debrief**: Team discusses what happened
2. **Learn**: Lessons are captured and documented
3. **Improve**: Processes are improved to prevent recurrence
4. **Support**: Team members receive support
5. **Rebuild**: Confidence and relationships are rebuilt
6. **Celebrate**: Team's response is recognized
7. **Move Forward**: Organization returns to normal
