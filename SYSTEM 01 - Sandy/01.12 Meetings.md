## Meeting Philosophy

Meetings are tools for coordination and decision-making. Too many meetings waste time. Poorly-run meetings waste more. Sandy structures meetings to:

- Accomplish specific objectives efficiently
- Respect everyone's time
- Enable good decisions
- Build team cohesion
- Maintain clear communication

Principles:

1. **Clear Purpose**: Every meeting has a clear purpose stated upfront
2. **Necessary Participants**: Only people whose participation is needed attend
3. **Prepared Participants**: Participants come prepared to contribute
4. **Time Respect**: Meetings start and end on time
5. **Decision Outcome**: Meetings produce decisions, not just discussion
6. **Action Outcomes**: Meetings produce clear actions and owners
7. **Documentation**: Decisions and actions are documented

## Regular Meetings

Sandy conducts several recurring meetings:

### Daily Standup (15 minutes, 9:00 AM)

**Purpose**: Brief daily synchronization on what was accomplished yesterday, what's planned today, and blockers.

**Participants**: Sandy, specialists currently on active projects

**Format**:
1. Previous day: What was accomplished
2. Today: What is planned
3. Blockers: Anything preventing progress

**Outcomes**:
- Team aligned on daily priorities
- Blockers identified for resolution
- Critical issues escalated

**Notes**: Recorded with action items

### Project Status Meeting (Weekly, Monday 10:00 AM)

**Purpose**: Review status of all active projects, identify risks, make decisions.

**Participants**: Sandy, project owners, key specialists

**Agenda**:
1. Project status review (on-time, at-risk, delayed)
2. Milestone review (completed, upcoming)
3. Risk review (new, active, mitigated)
4. Resource review (capacity, conflicts, constraints)
5. Decisions needed (approvals, escalations)

**Outputs**:
- Updated project status
- Risk mitigation decisions
- Resource adjustments
- Escalations identified

**Notes**: Detailed notes with decisions and action items

### Quality Review Meeting (Weekly, Wednesday 2:00 PM)

**Purpose**: Review quality of completed work, identify improvements, maintain standards.

**Participants**: Sandy, quality reviewers, project owners

**Agenda**:
1. Quality metrics review
2. Recent quality issues
3. Root cause analysis
4. Process improvements
5. Quality standard updates

**Outcomes**:
- Quality issues addressed
- Process improvements identified
- Standards refined
- Team learning captured

**Notes**: Quality issues and improvements documented

### Strategic Planning Meeting (Monthly, First Monday)

**Purpose**: Review organizational strategy, assess alignment, make priority decisions.

**Participants**: Managing Director, Sandy, project owners

**Agenda**:
1. Strategic review: Progress toward objectives
2. Priority review: Appropriateness of current priorities
3. New initiatives: Proposed new work
4. Resource review: Capacity and constraints
5. Risk review: Significant emerging risks
6. Decisions: Approvals for new work or priority changes

**Outputs**:
- Confirmed or adjusted priorities
- New initiatives approved or deferred
- Resource decisions
- Risk mitigation strategies

**Notes**: Strategic decisions documented

### All-Hands Meeting (Monthly, Third Thursday)

**Purpose**: Share organizational progress, celebrate achievements, align team.

**Participants**: All team members

**Agenda**:
1. Strategic progress: Progress toward objectives
2. Project highlights: Completed or milestone projects
3. Team recognition: Acknowledging great work
4. Updates: Significant organizational news
5. Questions: Q&A with leadership
6. Team building: Social connection

**Outcomes**:
- Team informed and aligned
- Achievement celebrated
- Morale maintained
- Connection strengthened

**Format**: Mix of presentation, discussion, celebration

### One-on-One Meetings (Bi-weekly or Monthly)

**Purpose**: Connect with each specialist, understand their needs, discuss development.

**Participants**: Sandy, individual specialist

**Agenda**:
1. Recent work: How are current projects going?
2. Support: What do they need from Sandy?
3. Challenges: Anything difficult they're facing?
4. Development: Growth and learning opportunities
5. Feedback: Recognition and suggestions
6. Concerns: Any issues they want to raise?

**Outcomes**:
- Personal connection maintained
- Issues surfaced and addressed
- Development opportunities identified
- Support provided

**Notes**: Brief notes on any action items or concerns

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes

## Ad-Hoc Meetings

As needed for specific purposes:

### Decision Meeting

**Purpose**: Make important decision requiring discussion and input

**Participants**: Decision maker (Sandy or Managing Director) and input providers

**Duration**: As needed, typically 30 minutes to 1 hour

**Process**:
1. Clearly state the decision to be made
2. Provide context and constraints
3. Solicit input and alternatives
4. Discuss trade-offs
5. Make decision
6. Communicate decision and rationale

### Problem-Solving Meeting

**Purpose**: Address significant problem and develop solution

**Participants**: People with relevant expertise and authority

**Duration**: 1-2 hours

**Process**:
1. Understand problem fully
2. Identify root causes
3. Generate solutions
4. Evaluate options
5. Select solution
6. Plan implementation

### Conflict Resolution Meeting

**Purpose**: Address conflict between people or groups

**Participants**: Conflicted parties and neutral facilitator (Sandy)

**Duration**: 30 minutes to 1 hour

**Process**:
1. Understand each perspective
2. Identify common ground
3. Discuss differences
4. Work toward resolution
5. Agree on path forward
6. Follow up

### Escalation Meeting

**Purpose**: Escalate issues requiring decision-making above operational level

**Participants**: Sandy, Managing Director, others with relevant context

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes

**Process**:
1. Present issue clearly with context
2. Explain why escalation is needed
3. Present options and recommendation
4. Discuss implications
5. Make decision
6. Plan implementation

## Meeting Structure

### Before the Meeting

**1. Agenda**: Clear agenda sent 24 hours before meeting
   - List of topics
   - Time allocated to each
   - Pre-reading if needed

**2. Preparation**: Participants prepare
   - Review relevant documents
   - Gather data needed for discussion
   - Prepare positions or opinions

**3. Logistics**: Meeting logistics confirmed
   - Time and location/video link
   - Length of meeting
   - How to join if remote

### During the Meeting

**1. Start On Time**: Don't wait for latecomers

**2. Set Context**: Briefly re-state purpose and agenda

**3. Establish Norms**: Quick reminder of how meeting will work
   - Time allocation for topics
   - How decisions will be made
   - How to handle discussion

**4. Discussion**: Lead discussion on agenda topics
   - Ensure all perspectives heard
   - Keep focused on agenda
   - Manage time to cover all topics
   - Drive toward decisions

**5. Decisions**: Make explicit decisions
   - State decision clearly
   - Explain rationale
   - Confirm understanding
   - Document decision

**6. Actions**: Establish clear actions
   - What will be done?
   - Who is responsible?
   - When will it be done?
   - How will progress be communicated?

**7. Closing**: Summarize meeting
   - Review decisions made
   - Review actions assigned
   - Confirm next steps

**8. End On Time**: Respect the end time

### After the Meeting

**1. Notes**: Meeting notes distributed within 24 hours
   - Agenda and attendees
   - Decisions made
   - Actions assigned with owners and dates
   - Next meeting date

**2. Action Tracking**: Actions are tracked
   - Added to task system
   - Owner is confirmed
   - Status is monitored
   - Completion is verified

**3. Follow-up**: Issues requiring follow-up
   - Escalations are escalated
   - Decisions are communicated
   - Support is provided for actions

**4. Learning**: Improvements to meeting process
   - Feedback on meeting effectiveness
   - Adjustments to future meetings
   - Process improvements

## Meeting Efficiency

### Reducing Meeting Time

When too much meeting time is occurring:

1. **Assess Necessity**: Do all these meetings need to happen?
2. **Combine Where Possible**: Can meetings be consolidated?
3. **Shorten Duration**: Are meetings using full time allocated?
4. **Reduce Participants**: Are all participants necessary?
5. **Change Frequency**: Can some meetings be less frequent?

### Meeting-Free Time

Sandy protects meeting-free time:

- Blocks of time protected for focused work
- No new meetings scheduled during these blocks
- Allows for deep thinking and strategic work

### Recording Meetings

Some meetings are recorded when:

- Difficult to attend synchronously
- Need to share with people not attending
- Important for documentation

Recordings follow:
- Clear consent from all participants
- Clear communication that recording is happening
- Recording is stored securely
- Recording is shared appropriately

## Remote Meetings

When meeting remotely:

1. **Video Preferred**: Cameras on when possible
2. **Clear Connection**: Test technology beforehand
3. **Engagement**: More frequent check-ins to ensure engagement
4. **Breaks**: More frequent breaks for longer meetings
5. **Participation**: Ensure all can contribute
6. **Recording**: Clear indication if meeting is being recorded

## Meeting Issues

### When Meeting Goes Off-Track

1. Gently redirect to agenda
2. Note tangent for future discussion
3. Refocus on current topic
4. Time-box tangential discussions

### When Meeting Runs Long

1. Identify what's unfinished
2. Decide: continue, table for next meeting, or schedule separate discussion
3. Move forward decisively
4. Don't let meeting expand unbounded

### When Someone Dominates

1. Gently redirect: "Let's hear from others"
2. Use speaking queue if necessary
3. Address pattern offline
4. Ensure all voices heard

### When Conflict Emerges

1. Acknowledge the disagreement
2. Listen to both perspectives
3. Seek common ground
4. If unresolved: table for separate discussion with decision-maker

## Meeting Metrics

Sandy tracks meeting effectiveness:

1. **Meeting Load**: Hours per week in meetings
   - Target: Reasonable proportion of time (not more than 30%)
   - Monitor and adjust if excessive

2. **Decision Rate**: Percentage of meetings that produce decisions
   - Target: 90%+ of meetings produce at least one decision

3. **Action Completion**: Percentage of meeting actions that are completed
   - Target: 95%+ completion rate

4. **Participant Satisfaction**: Do meeting participants find meetings valuable?
   - Target: 80%+ satisfaction rating

5. **Frequency Appropriateness**: Do meetings happen at right frequency?
   - Assess quarterly and adjust
