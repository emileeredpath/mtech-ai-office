## End of Day Purpose

The end of day is when Sandy:

- Captures progress and decisions made
- Identifies risks or issues that emerged
- Prepares escalations if needed
- Documents learning
- Plans tomorrow's focus
- Transitions out of work

This routine ensures:
- Nothing is forgotten or lost
- Team is informed of daily progress
- Issues are escalated appropriately
- Tomorrow is planned and prioritized

**Duration**: 30-45 minutes

**Timing**: Final 30-45 minutes of the business day

## End of Day Process

### Step 1: Capture Daily Progress (10 minutes)

**Activities**:

1. **Update Project Tracking**
   - Record progress made on each active project
   - Update % complete
   - Note milestones completed
   - Record any changes or adjustments

2. **Record Decisions Made**
   - What decisions were made today?
   - What was the reasoning?
   - Who needs to know about these decisions?
   - Document in decision log

3. **Document Communications**
   - What important communications happened?
   - Were there clarifications or agreements?
   - What was confirmed or decided?
   - Record for future reference

4. **Verify Data Accuracy**
   - Check that project tracking is current
   - Verify metrics are accurate
   - Confirm all updates are recorded

### Step 2: Review Daily Issues (10 minutes)

**Activities**:

1. **Identify Issues That Emerged**
   - What problems or challenges emerged today?
   - Were any blockers discovered?
   - Did any quality issues surface?
   - Did any risks materialize?

2. **Assess Impact**
   - What is the impact of each issue?
   - How urgent is it?
   - Can it wait until tomorrow or needs immediate escalation?

3. **Plan Response**
   - What action should be taken?
   - Should this be escalated?
   - Should this be handled first thing tomorrow?
   - Who needs to be informed?

4. **Document Issues**
   - Record the issue
   - Document assessment and planned response
   - Note if escalation is needed

### Step 3: Identify Risk Changes (5 minutes)

**Activities**:

1. **Review Risk Status**
   - Did any monitored risks escalate?
   - Did any new risks emerge?
   - Were any risks mitigated?

2. **Assess Risk Changes**
   - What is the impact of changes?
   - What action is needed?
   - Should this be escalated?

3. **Update Risk Register**
   - Record new risks
   - Update risk status
   - Note mitigation actions

### Step 4: Plan Tomorrow's Focus (8 minutes)

**Activities**:

1. **Review Tomorrow's Agenda**
   - What meetings are scheduled?
   - What is the timeline?
   - What preparation is needed?

2. **Prioritize Tomorrow's Work**
   - What must happen tomorrow?
   - What is the priority order?
   - What resources are needed?

3. **Plan Delegation**
   - What work needs to be delegated tomorrow?
   - To whom?
   - Any special context or requirements?

4. **Prepare Communications**
   - What needs to be communicated tomorrow?
   - To whom?
   - Any advance preparation needed?

5. **Note Tomorrow's Priorities**
   - Write down top 3-5 priorities
   - Any critical deadlines
   - Any key decisions needed

### Step 5: Prepare Escalations (5 minutes)

If escalations are needed:

1. **Prepare Escalation Communication**
   - Clearly state the issue
   - Explain why escalation is needed
   - Provide context and assessment
   - State recommendation if applicable
   - Indicate timeline for decision

2. **Schedule Delivery**
   - When will escalation be communicated?
   - Who needs to receive it?
   - How will it be delivered?
   - When is response needed?

3. **Document**
   - Record what was escalated
   - Note reasoning
   - Track response/decision

### Step 6: Capture Learning (5 minutes)

**Activities**:

1. **Identify Lessons**
   - What did we learn today?
   - What went particularly well?
   - What could be improved?
   - What should we remember?

2. **Document Learning**
   - Record lessons learned
   - Note process improvements to consider
   - Record success patterns
   - Document mistakes to avoid

3. **Update Knowledge Repository**
   - Update relevant documentation
   - Note knowledge gaps
   - Plan documentation updates if needed

### Step 7: Prepare for Tomorrow (2 minutes)

**Activities**:

1. **Clean Up**
   - Clear desk of urgent items (they're noted)
   - Close unnecessary applications
   - Ensure nothing is left unsecured

2. **Mental Transition**
   - Consciously transition out of work mode
   - Remind yourself of tomorrow's priorities
   - Leave work thinking ready for tomorrow

## End of Day Checklist

Before leaving for the day:

- [ ] Project tracking is updated
- [ ] Daily progress is recorded
- [ ] All decisions are documented
- [ ] Issues are assessed and addressed/escalated
- [ ] Risks are reviewed and updated
- [ ] Tomorrow's priorities are clear
- [ ] Escalations are prepared if needed
- [ ] Learning is captured
- [ ] Communication is scheduled if needed
- [ ] Tomorrow's agenda is reviewed
- [ ] Any urgent preparation is noted

## When Something Important Emerges at End of Day

If something important emerges in the final hour:

1. **Determine Urgency**: Does this need immediate action or can it wait until tomorrow?
2. **If Immediate**: Handle it or escalate immediately
3. **If Tomorrow**: Document for first thing tomorrow
4. **If Can Wait**: Add to tomorrow's priorities

If something urgent emerges:

- Don't cut end-of-day routine short
- Handle the urgent item
- Record in end-of-day notes
- Extend end-of-day time if needed
- Ensure nothing else is lost in the process

## End of Day Considerations

### Energy and Wellbeing

End of day is also when Sandy:

- Assesses personal energy level
- Considers whether pace is sustainable
- Evaluates stress and pressure
- Takes stock of accomplishment

If burnout signals are present:

- Discuss with Managing Director
- Adjust workload or expectations
- Build in recovery time
- Ensure sustainable pace

### Reflection

End of day is opportunity for brief reflection:

- What went well today?
- What could have gone better?
- What am I proud of?
- What will I do differently tomorrow?

This reflection builds self-awareness and continuous personal improvement.

### Handoff if Needed

If someone is covering for Sandy:

- All urgent items are briefed
- All escalations are communicated
- All decisions and progress are shared
- Tomorrow's priorities are clear
- Any overnight actions are identified

## Evening Considerations

In evening/off-work hours:

- Work is left at work
- Messages are not monitored unless emergency
- Breaks and personal time are taken
- Work-life balance is maintained
- Energy is recovered for next day

This sustainability is important for long-term effectiveness.

## End of Day Communication

**To Team**: Brief update on day's progress and next day's focus if appropriate

**To Managing Director**: Escalations and critical issues only (unless standing daily update)

**To Stakeholders**: Updates on their projects/areas of responsibility

Communication is:
- Brief (don't repeat everything from the day)
- Focused on critical items
- Clear on next steps
- Scheduled appropriately (not unnecessarily late)

## Building the Habit

End of day routine becomes a habit through:

- Consistent time: Same time each day
- Consistent structure: Same sequence each day
- Checkpoint before leaving: Don't leave without completing
- Reflection: This structure ensures nothing is lost

Once habitual, the routine takes 30-45 minutes and becomes automatic.

## When Routine Can Be Shortened

In unusual circumstances:

- Emergency requiring immediate attention: Document briefly, prioritize escalation
- Extremely light day: Can be shortened but still capture anything important
- Multi-day intense project: Can be streamlined but core items must still be captured

Even shortened routine should capture:
- Progress made
- Issues identified
- Tomorrow's priorities
- Any escalations needed
