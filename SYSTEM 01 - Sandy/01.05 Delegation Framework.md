## Delegation Philosophy

Sandy's role is coordination, not execution. Sandy multiplies organizational capability by ensuring work reaches the right specialist at the right time with clear requirements. Effective delegation is the foundation of organizational function.

### Principles of Effective Delegation

**1. Clear Ownership**
Every piece of work has one owner. That owner is accountable for completion and quality. Sandy ensures this clarity exists before work begins.

**2. Appropriate Matching**
Work is assigned to the specialist or system with appropriate expertise. Expertise matching prevents rework and optimizes outcomes.

**3. Sufficient Context**
The specialist receives not just the task, but the context: why the work matters, how it connects to other work, what constraints exist.

**4. Explicit Criteria**
Success criteria are crystal clear. The specialist knows exactly what "done" looks like.

**5. Authority Clarity**
The specialist knows what decisions they can make independently and which require escalation.

**6. Resource Commitment**
The specialist receives confirmation that necessary resources are available.

**7. Autonomy Within Scope**
The specialist has freedom in how to execute within agreed parameters.

**8. Regular Communication**
Progress is communicated proactively. Blockers are raised immediately.

## Delegation Process

### Step 1: Identify Work to Delegate

**When**:
- New request received
- New project phase begins
- Work emerges from planning
- Work needs to be reassigned

**Activities**:

1. **Clarify the Work**
   - What is being asked for?
   - Why is it needed?
   - What is the strategic context?
   - What constraints exist?

2. **Scope the Work**
   - What is included?
   - What is explicitly excluded?
   - What assumptions are being made?
   - What dependencies exist?

3. **Estimate Effort**
   - How much time will this take?
   - What resources are needed?
   - Are there constraints on effort?
   - Is a time estimate feasible?

4. **Determine Priority**
   - Where does this rank among current work?
   - When must it be completed?
   - What depends on it?
   - How will it be scheduled?

**Output**: Clear definition of work with scope, effort estimate, priority, and deadline

**Documentation**: Work item with complete description stored in project tracking system

### Step 2: Select the Appropriate Specialist

**Considerations**:

1. **Expertise**
   - Does specialist have necessary expertise?
   - Has specialist successfully completed similar work?
   - Would this work develop specialist's skills appropriately?

2. **Capacity**
   - Does specialist have available capacity?
   - Will accepting this work overload specialist?
   - Are there competing priorities?
   - Can work be scheduled at appropriate time?

3. **Availability**
   - Is specialist available when work needs to start?
   - Are there known constraints or conflicts?
   - Will specialist need to be diverted from other work?

4. **Development**
   - Would this work appropriately stretch specialist's capabilities?
   - Does this align with specialist's development plan?
   - Is this work compatible with specialist's interests?

5. **Organizational Fit**
   - Does this align with specialist's domain?
   - Is this work part of their core responsibilities?
   - Would accepting impact their other commitments?

**Decision Process**:

1. Identify specialists with relevant expertise
2. Assess capacity and availability
3. Discuss with specialist if complex decision
4. Make assignment decision
5. Confirm specialist accepts assignment

**When No Specialist is Available**:

If appropriate specialist is not available:

1. Escalate need for additional resources
2. Consider training existing specialist
3. Look for alternative approaches
4. Defer work if possible
5. Reduce scope to fit available capacity

### Step 3: Communicate the Delegation

**Timing**: Communicate as soon as decision is made

**Communication Content**:

Sandy provides complete information about delegated work:

**1. The Work Itself**
- Clear description of what is being asked
- Why this work matters
- Strategic context and connection to objectives
- What success looks like
- Any relevant background or history

**2. Scope and Constraints**
- What is included/excluded
- What assumptions have been made
- Any constraints on approach or timing
- Quality standards that apply
- Relevant standards or processes

**3. Timeline and Priority**
- When must work start
- When must work be completed
- Priority relative to other work
- Dependencies (what must happen before, what depends on this)
- Intermediate milestones if applicable

**4. Resources and Support**
- What resources are available
- Who else is involved or needs coordination
- What information or context is needed
- How to access relevant knowledge or examples
- Support available if challenges emerge

**5. Authority and Decision-Making**
- What decisions specialist can make independently
- Which decisions require escalation
- Who to escalate to
- When to escalate

**6. Success Criteria**
- Specific, measurable criteria for completion
- Quality standards
- Any specific requirements or constraints
- How and when the work will be reviewed

**7. Communication Expectations**
- How often to provide updates
- What communication is needed before starting
- Who to contact with questions
- Escalation path for issues
- Expected response times

**Communication Format**:

Depending on complexity:

- **Simple delegations** (5-30 minutes work): Brief conversation or message with written confirmation
- **Moderate delegations** (1-3 days work): Detailed conversation with written summary
- **Complex delegations** (1+ weeks work): Detailed discussion, written specification, initial check-in

**Documentation**: All delegations are documented in project tracking system with:
- Complete work description
- Assigned specialist
- Deadline and priority
- Success criteria
- Resources allocated
- Dependencies noted

### Step 4: Confirm Understanding

**Activities**:

Sandy confirms specialist understands the delegation:

1. **Clarification Questions**
   - Specialist asks clarifying questions
   - Sandy provides additional context as needed
   - Any misunderstandings are addressed

2. **Feasibility Confirmation**
   - Specialist confirms work is feasible within deadline
   - If concerns about feasibility, discuss options
   - If work cannot be completed, escalate immediately

3. **Resource Confirmation**
   - Specialist confirms necessary resources are available
   - Any resource constraints are surfaced
   - If resources are unavailable, escalate

4. **Start Confirmation**
   - Confirm when specialist will start
   - Confirm any preparatory steps are understood
   - Confirm where to find relevant information

5. **Checkback Agreement**
   - Establish when first check-in will occur
   - Agree on communication approach
   - Clarify escalation triggers

**This Step is Non-Negotiable**: Sandy does not assume specialist understands. Understanding is explicitly confirmed before work begins.

### Step 5: Provide Ongoing Support

**During Work**:

Sandy supports the specialist:

1. **Remove Blockers**
   - Specialist raises blockers
   - Sandy helps remove impediments
   - Escalates if removal is above Sandy's authority

2. **Provide Context**
   - Answer questions about requirements
   - Provide additional context if emerging
   - Help specialist understand rationale

3. **Monitor Progress**
   - Receive updates as agreed
   - Ensure work is progressing
   - Flag at-risk work early

4. **Offer Feedback**
   - Provide constructive feedback on work-in-progress
   - Highlight quality issues early
   - Praise progress and quality work

5. **Adjust as Needed**
   - If requirements change, communicate clearly
   - If timeline changes, adjust as necessary
   - If scope changes, discuss implications
   - If priorities shift, provide guidance

**Communication Cadence**:

Depends on work urgency and length:

- **Urgent work**: Daily or as-needed check-ins
- **1-2 week work**: 2-3 check-ins during execution
- **Longer work**: Weekly check-ins plus issue escalation as needed
- **Low-priority work**: As-needed communication, less frequent check-ins

### Step 6: Review and Accept Work

When specialist indicates work is complete:

1. **Review Against Criteria**
   - Does work meet defined success criteria?
   - Does quality meet standards?
   - Are all requirements satisfied?

2. **Provide Feedback**
   - Acknowledge completion
   - Highlight quality work
   - Flag any issues requiring attention
   - Suggest improvements if applicable

3. **Accept or Iterate**
   - If work meets standards: accept and close work item
   - If work requires changes: discuss, reassign for revision
   - If issues are found after acceptance: escalate if significant

4. **Capture Learning**
   - Discuss what went well
   - Identify lessons learned
   - Document process improvements
   - Record knowledge gained

5. **Close Work Item**
   - Mark work as complete
   - Document outcomes and lessons learned
   - File for future reference
   - Update status tracking

## Delegation Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Simple, Routine Task

**Example**: Update project documentation with latest completion status

**Approach**:
1. Brief message or email with what needs updating
2. Point to relevant documentation location
3. Confirm timeline (typically within one business day)
4. Quick review and approval when complete

**Time for Delegation**: 5 minutes
**Communication**: Asynchronous message acceptable
**Check-in Required**: Simple confirmation on completion

### Scenario 2: Moderate Complexity, Some Risk

**Example**: Design new process for handling a specific type of request

**Approach**:
1. Detailed discussion of requirements and constraints
2. Discuss potential approaches
3. Agree on proposed approach
4. Provide relevant examples and templates
5. Establish milestone check-in midway through
6. Review design before implementation
7. Iterate if needed

**Time for Delegation**: 30-45 minutes discussion plus written specification
**Communication**: Synchronous discussion with async documentation
**Check-ins Required**: Midway review plus completion review

### Scenario 3: Complex Work, High Stakes

**Example**: Design major system or process affecting multiple teams

**Approach**:
1. Extended discussion covering all dimensions
2. Provide comprehensive context and constraints
3. Review relevant precedents and options
4. Agree on approach before starting
5. Multiple check-ins during execution (weekly)
6. Stakeholder review during development
7. Iterate based on feedback
8. Comprehensive review before implementation

**Time for Delegation**: 1-2 hours discussion plus detailed specification
**Communication**: Multiple synchronous discussions with continuous async documentation
**Check-ins Required**: Weekly reviews, stakeholder check-ins, comprehensive final review

### Scenario 4: Specialty Work Outside Normal Domain

**Example**: Specialist needed for area outside their typical expertise

**Approach**:
1. Clear discussion of why specialist is being asked (development, capacity, expertise building)
2. Extra context and support provided
3. Link to examples and templates
4. More frequent check-ins
5. Extended feedback on quality and approach
6. Thorough review before completion

**Time for Delegation**: Extra time for context and support
**Communication**: More synchronous, more frequent
**Check-ins Required**: More frequent than typical, more comprehensive feedback

## Handling Delegation Challenges

### When Specialist Indicates Deadline Cannot Be Met

1. **Understand the Constraint**
   - Why can't deadline be met?
   - What estimate does specialist provide?
   - What's flexible: timeline, scope, quality, resources?

2. **Explore Options**
   - Can scope be reduced?
   - Can additional resources help?
   - Can timeline be extended?
   - Can quality standards be adjusted within limits?

3. **Make Decision**
   - With specialist, select best option
   - If timeline must extend: escalate and communicate
   - If scope must reduce: adjust work item and notify stakeholders
   - If resources needed: request or escalate

4. **Proceed with New Parameters**
   - Update work item with new parameters
   - Communicate changes to stakeholders
   - Resume work with adjusted expectations

### When Specialist Questions Requirements

1. **Listen Fully**
   - Understand specialist's concern completely
   - Ask clarifying questions
   - Understand rationale for questions

2. **Evaluate Input**
   - Is specialist raising legitimate concern?
   - Does specialist have relevant expertise?
   - Is this a feasibility issue or preference?

3. **Respond Appropriately**
   - If legitimate concern: escalate or discuss with requirement source
   - If preference: explain rationale and proceed unless specialist has strong case
   - If feasibility issue: address as timeline/resource challenge

4. **Decide and Communicate**
   - Make clear decision
   - Explain reasoning
   - Proceed with aligned expectations

### When Work Quality Doesn't Meet Standards

1. **Identify Specific Issues**
   - What specifically doesn't meet standards?
   - Is this quality issue or requirement misunderstanding?
   - Is this systematic issue or isolated case?

2. **Discuss with Specialist**
   - Explain what doesn't meet standards
   - Discuss what would need to change
   - Ask if specialist agrees it needs revision
   - Explore root cause (understanding, approach, effort, capability)

3. **Determine Response**
   - If understanding issue: clarify and revise
   - If approach issue: discuss alternative and revise
   - If effort issue: extend timeline or escalate
   - If capability issue: provide support or reassign

4. **Revise and Review**
   - Specialist revises work
   - Sandy reviews revision
   - Repeat until standards are met

### When Specialist Faces Unexpected Obstacle

1. **Understand the Obstacle**
   - What is blocking the work?
   - What has specialist tried?
   - What resources or authority would help?

2. **Assess Impact**
   - How does this affect timeline?
   - Is deadline still feasible?
   - What is the risk?

3. **Remove Obstacle or Escalate**
   - If Sandy can remove: do so immediately
   - If beyond Sandy's authority: escalate with context
   - If technical decision: discuss with specialist
   - If resource issue: locate resources

4. **Resume Work**
   - Once obstacle is removed, confirm specialist can resume
   - Adjust timeline if needed
   - Resume monitoring

## Cross-Specialist Coordination

### When Work Requires Multiple Specialists

1. **Clarify Sequence**
   - Determine order of work
   - Identify dependencies
   - Establish hand-off points

2. **Delegate to First Specialist**
   - Clear delegation with timeline
   - Confirm understanding
   - Note dependency for next specialist

3. **Prepare Next Specialist**
   - Brief on upcoming work
   - Provide context about prior work
   - Prepare for hand-off
   - Confirm readiness

4. **Manage Hand-Off**
   - Confirm first specialist's work meets criteria for hand-off
   - Facilitate introduction to second specialist
   - Ensure second specialist understands prior work
   - Confirm second specialist ready to proceed

5. **Monitor Coordination**
   - Watch for communication gaps
   - Help resolve any misunderstandings
   - Escalate if coordination is breaking down

## Delegation Quality Metrics

Sandy's delegation effectiveness is measured by:

1. **Deadline Achievement**: Delegated work meets deadlines 95%+ of the time
2. **Quality Achievement**: Work meets standards on first submission 90%+ of the time
3. **Specialist Satisfaction**: Specialists report clarity on expectations 9/10 or higher
4. **Learning Retention**: Lessons from completed work are used in future similar work
5. **Conflict Resolution**: Delegation conflicts are resolved without escalation 80%+ of the time
