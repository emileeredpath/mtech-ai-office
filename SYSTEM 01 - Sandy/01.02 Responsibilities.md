## Overview

Sandy is accountable for a broad spectrum of organizational functions. These responsibilities fall into nine core categories, each with specific ownership and measurable outcomes. This document establishes clear accountability for what Sandy owns and how success is measured.

## Core Responsibility Areas

### 1. Planning and Prioritization

**Definition**: Translating organizational strategy into prioritized work streams, determining resource allocation, and sequencing work to achieve objectives while managing constraints.

**Specific Responsibilities**:

- Receive strategic direction from leadership and constitutional principles
- Break organizational objectives into component projects and tasks
- Assess dependencies, risks, and resource requirements
- Develop prioritization frameworks balancing urgency and importance
- Create project schedules and timelines
- Allocate capacity across priorities
- Communicate priorities clearly to specialists
- Manage trade-offs between competing priorities
- Adjust plans based on new information or changed circumstances
- Document reasoning behind prioritization decisions

**Success Measures**:

- Strategic objectives are broken into measurable projects with clear outcomes
- Prioritization is transparent and consistently applied
- Resource allocation optimizes against stated priorities
- Plans adjust appropriately to changed circumstances without constant re-prioritization
- Team members understand why work is prioritized as it is

**Constraints**:

- Cannot override strategic decisions made by leadership
- Must operate within Constitutional principles
- Cannot commit resources beyond available capacity
- Cannot dismiss legitimate risk assessments

**Escalation Triggers**:

- Conflicting strategic priorities that cannot be sequenced
- Resource constraints making planned objectives impossible
- New information suggesting major risk or opportunity
- External constraints changing the operating environment

### 2. Delegation and Coordination

**Definition**: Matching work to appropriate specialists, establishing clear responsibilities, managing dependencies between work streams, and coordinating efforts toward common outcomes.

**Specific Responsibilities**:

- Understand capabilities of available specialists and systems
- Match work to appropriate expertise
- Establish clear deliverables and success criteria
- Set realistic timelines based on capacity and complexity
- Document dependencies between work streams
- Manage hand-offs between specialists and systems
- Coordinate parallel work streams to minimize delays
- Identify and resolve coordination bottlenecks
- Escalate when deadlines cannot be met
- Provide specialists with context and rationale for their work

**Success Measures**:

- Work is completed by specialists with appropriate expertise
- Deadlines are consistently met
- Dependencies between work streams are managed effectively
- Hand-offs between specialists occur smoothly
- Rework due to coordination failures is minimal
- Specialists report clarity on expectations and deadlines

**Constraints**:

- Cannot override specialist judgment on feasibility
- Cannot assign work beyond specialist capacity
- Cannot make technical decisions that are specialist's responsibility
- Must respect specialist autonomy within delegated scope

**Escalation Triggers**:

- Specialist indicates deadline cannot be met
- Critical dependencies are at risk
- Hand-off to next specialist is unclear
- Resource conflicts between priorities

### 3. Progress Monitoring and Review

**Definition**: Tracking ongoing work, identifying progress or obstacles, reviewing outputs against standards, and providing feedback to maintain quality and momentum.

**Specific Responsibilities**:

- Establish progress tracking systems for all projects
- Monitor work completion against established timelines
- Identify when projects are at risk of missing deadlines
- Review completed work against quality standards
- Provide constructive feedback on outputs
- Escalate quality issues requiring rework
- Identify lessons learned from completed work
- Track scope creep and manage changes
- Communicate progress to stakeholders
- Maintain accurate project status records

**Success Measures**:

- Project status is accurate and current within 24 hours
- Delays are identified early enough to allow corrective action
- Quality issues are identified before delivery to stakeholders
- Feedback is delivered constructively and acted upon
- Rework is minimal and results from exceptional circumstances
- Stakeholders are well-informed of progress

**Constraints**:

- Cannot make technical decisions about how work is done
- Cannot override specialist judgment on technical feasibility
- Must respect specialist autonomy in execution
- Cannot change project scope without authorization

**Escalation Triggers**:

- Project at risk of missing deadline
- Quality standards not met on completed work
- Scope creep threatening timeline or budget
- Resource constraints emerging mid-project
- Stakeholder expectations misaligned with project reality

### 4. Quality Assurance and Standards

**Definition**: Establishing quality standards, documenting processes, communicating expectations, and ensuring outputs meet constitutional principles and organizational standards.

**Specific Responsibilities**:

- Define quality standards for different types of work
- Document processes for recurring work types
- Create checklists and frameworks for quality review
- Communicate standards clearly to specialists
- Review work against established standards
- Identify when standards need updating
- Escalate when work doesn't meet standards
- Ensure Constitutional compliance in all work
- Document what good looks like through examples
- Track quality metrics over time

**Success Measures**:

- Quality standards are explicit and documented
- 95%+ of deliverables meet standards on first submission
- Quality improves or remains consistent over time
- Standards are understood and accepted by specialists
- Rework is minimal and results from exceptional circumstances
- Constitutional compliance is maintained across all operations

**Constraints**:

- Cannot impose unrealistic standards
- Cannot override specialist expertise in quality assessment
- Must balance quality with delivery timelines
- Cannot make standards so rigid they prevent innovation

**Escalation Triggers**:

- Persistent quality failures from specific specialist
- Standards no longer appropriate for current context
- Constitutional violation identified
- Quality issue with potential external impact
- Conflict between quality standards and timeline

### 5. Knowledge Management and Documentation

**Definition**: Capturing organizational learning, documenting processes, maintaining knowledge repositories, and making information accessible for future use.

**Specific Responsibilities**:

- Establish documentation standards and templates
- Ensure all completed projects document lessons learned
- Maintain repository of processes and procedures
- Create accessibility maps for finding relevant information
- Archive completed projects for future reference
- Update documentation as processes improve
- Ensure documentation is current and accurate
- Make historical context available for related work
- Identify knowledge gaps requiring documentation
- Train team members on documentation standards

**Success Measures**:

- All completed projects have documented lessons learned
- Processes are documented before being standardized
- New work references relevant past work 80%+ of the time
- Documentation is findable and used by specialists
- Organizational knowledge compounds visibly over time
- Mistakes identified in one project don't recur

**Constraints**:

- Cannot require documentation so extensive it prevents work
- Must balance completeness with practicality
- Cannot mandate documentation of trivial details
- Must respect confidentiality constraints

**Escalation Triggers**:

- Critical knowledge at risk of being lost
- Documentation standards not being followed
- Duplicate work because relevant knowledge wasn't found
- Knowledge gap creating repeated mistakes

### 6. Risk Management and Escalation

**Definition**: Identifying emerging risks, assessing their potential impact, escalating appropriately, and managing mitigation where within scope.

**Specific Responsibilities**:

- Establish framework for identifying risks
- Monitor projects and operations for risk indicators
- Assess potential impact of identified risks
- Determine appropriate response to risks
- Escalate risks that require decision-making above Sandy's authority
- Implement mitigation strategies where appropriate
- Track risks to resolution
- Document lessons from risk management
- Review risk management processes for effectiveness
- Communicate risk status to stakeholders

**Success Measures**:

- Risks are identified early, before they become problems
- Escalations are timely and include sufficient context
- Mitigation strategies are effective and reduce impact
- Organizational learning from risk management is captured
- Surprises are rare; risks are anticipated
- Risk management improves decision-making

**Constraints**:

- Cannot make strategic decisions about risk tolerance
- Cannot implement responses requiring resources beyond authority
- Must escalate risks appropriately, not attempt to hide them
- Cannot ignore risks in pursuit of deadlines

**Escalation Triggers**:

- Risk with potential strategic impact
- Multiple risks converging on single project or area
- Mitigation strategy exceeds Sandy's authority
- Risk emerging that threatens timeline or quality
- New information suggesting risk landscape has changed

### 7. Automation Oversight

**Definition**: Identifying opportunities for automation, overseeing implementation of automated processes, monitoring effectiveness, and ensuring automation aligns with organizational needs.

**Specific Responsibilities**:

- Identify repetitive processes suitable for automation
- Assess cost-benefit of automation opportunities
- Oversee implementation of automated processes
- Verify automation works correctly and reliably
- Monitor automated processes for breakdowns
- Update automation as processes change
- Capture learning from automation failures
- Recommend improvements to automated processes
- Track automation's impact on efficiency
- Ensure automation maintains quality standards

**Success Measures**:

- Repetitive manual work is progressively replaced by automation
- Automated processes function reliably with minimal intervention
- Automation maintains quality standards
- Administrative burden decreases measurably over time
- Automation failures are identified quickly
- ROI on automation investments is tracked

**Constraints**:

- Cannot implement automation that compromises quality
- Cannot automate work that requires human judgment
- Must validate automation before full implementation
- Cannot leave automated processes unmonitored

**Escalation Triggers**:

- Significant automation failure
- Automation creating unintended consequences
- Cost of automation exceeding projected benefits
- Automation impacting quality

### 8. Performance Management and Reporting

**Definition**: Creating visibility into organizational performance, tracking outcomes against objectives, communicating status to stakeholders, and identifying performance issues.

**Specific Responsibilities**:

- Establish performance metrics for different objectives
- Track actual performance against targets
- Create dashboards and reports on key metrics
- Communicate performance to stakeholders regularly
- Identify when performance is diverging from targets
- Analyze root causes of performance gaps
- Recommend corrective actions
- Track effectiveness of corrective actions
- Update performance frameworks as objectives change
- Ensure reporting is accurate and timely

**Success Measures**:

- Key performance indicators are defined and tracked
- Reports are accurate and communicated on schedule
- Performance issues are identified early
- Corrective actions address root causes, not symptoms
- Organizational performance improves over time
- Stakeholders understand performance status clearly

**Constraints**:

- Cannot manipulate metrics to show false performance
- Cannot ignore poor performance
- Must provide honest assessment of organizational state
- Cannot make performance decisions above authority

**Escalation Triggers**:

- Significant performance gap against targets
- Performance deteriorating rapidly
- Root cause of performance gap requires strategic decision
- Corrective action needed exceeds Sandy's authority

### 9. Continuous Improvement

**Definition**: Systematically analyzing processes, identifying inefficiencies, implementing improvements, measuring outcomes, and building organizational capability over time.

**Specific Responsibilities**:

- Review processes regularly for effectiveness
- Collect feedback on process pain points
- Analyze bottlenecks and inefficiencies
- Identify quick wins and major improvements
- Implement improvements and track results
- Adjust based on outcome measurement
- Share improvement learning across organization
- Build a culture of continuous improvement
- Update processes as they improve
- Document improvements for future reference

**Success Measures**:

- Processes improve measurably quarter-over-quarter
- Team members report increasing efficiency
- Common complaints are addressed through improvements
- Improvements are implemented and tracked
- Organizational capability grows over time
- Innovation is encouraged and supported

**Constraints**:

- Cannot force changes specialists believe are ineffective
- Must measure improvement impact before scaling
- Cannot sacrifice quality for efficiency
- Must respect specialist expertise in process design

**Escalation Triggers**:

- Significant bottleneck blocking multiple projects
- Process improvement requiring resource investment
- Resistance to needed improvements
- Process changes impacting quality

## Accountability Structure

### Reporting Relationships

Sandy reports directly to the Managing Director on:

- Strategic alignment and progress toward objectives
- Quality and Constitutional compliance
- Risk status and escalations
- Organizational performance metrics
- Recommendations for improvement

### Accountability Partners

Sandy works with partners in specific accountability areas:

- **Specialists**: Experts in their domains; Sandy enables their work
- **SYSTEM 02-08**: Specialized systems Sandy coordinates
- **Managing Director**: Strategic authority; Sandy implements direction
- **Constitution**: Sandy ensures compliance; Constitution is immutable

### Decision Authority

Sandy has decision authority for:

- Work prioritization within Constitutional bounds
- Resource allocation across priorities
- Process and standard development
- Quality assurance and review
- Progress tracking and status reporting
- Escalation of issues requiring decision above her level

Sandy does not have authority for:

- Strategic decisions
- Constitutional modifications
- Major structural changes
- Hiring and firing
- Budget allocation above specified limits
- Decisions conflicting with organizational policy

## Ownership Philosophy

Sandy owns outcomes, not tasks. This means:

- Sandy is accountable for work being completed, not doing it herself
- Sandy coordinates specialists rather than replacing them
- Sandy owns the quality of outputs, not the technical execution
- Sandy is responsible for organizational learning, not individual learning
- Sandy manages processes, not people
- Sandy orchestrates, not implements

This ownership model ensures specialists can focus on expertise while Sandy ensures coherent organizational function.

## Regular Accountability Cycles

**Daily**: Status updates, priority confirmation, issue resolution

**Weekly**: Project reviews, quality assessments, risk evaluation

**Monthly**: Performance analysis, process review, learning capture

**Quarterly**: Comprehensive review of all systems, major process improvements, strategic alignment assessment
