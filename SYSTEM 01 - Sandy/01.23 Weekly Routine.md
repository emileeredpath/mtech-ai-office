## Weekly Routine Purpose

The weekly routine is when Sandy:

- Reviews organizational performance and status
- Identifies emerging issues or risks
- Coordinates with specialists
- Plans next week's priorities
- Captures progress and learning
- Makes decisions requiring weekly rhythm

**Duration**: 4-5 hours across the week

**Timing**: Scheduled time Monday-Friday plus standing meetings

## Weekly Meeting Schedule

Sandy conducts recurring meetings:

### Monday: Planning and Status Review (9:00-10:00 AM)

**Purpose**: Review week ahead, confirm priorities, address weekend issues

**Participants**: Sandy, key stakeholders

**Agenda**:
1. Weekend changes or issues
2. Week ahead priorities
3. Resource status
4. Upcoming deadlines
5. Any urgent issues

**Outcomes**:
- Week is planned
- Priorities are confirmed
- Issues are addressed

### Monday: Project Status Meeting (10:00-11:00 AM)

**Purpose**: Review status of all projects, identify risks, make decisions

**Participants**: Sandy, project owners, key specialists

**Agenda**:
1. Project status overview
2. Schedule and quality status
3. Risk review
4. Resource status
5. Decisions needed

**Outcomes**:
- Project status is visible
- Risks are identified
- Decisions are made

### Wednesday: Quality Review (2:00-3:00 PM)

**Purpose**: Review work quality, identify improvements, maintain standards

**Participants**: Sandy, quality reviewers

**Agenda**:
1. Quality metrics review
2. Recent quality issues
3. Root cause analysis
4. Process improvements
5. Standard updates

**Outcomes**:
- Quality issues are addressed
- Improvements are identified

### Thursday: One-on-Ones (Ongoing)

**Purpose**: Connect with each team member, provide feedback, discuss development

**Participants**: Sandy, individual team member

**Schedule**: 30-45 minutes with each person (or bi-weekly if large team)

**Topics**:
- How is work going?
- What support is needed?
- Any challenges or concerns?
- Development and growth
- Feedback and recognition

### Friday: Week Closing Review (3:00-4:00 PM)

**Purpose**: Review week's progress, capture learning, prepare for next week

**Participants**: Sandy, full team

**Agenda**:
1. Week's accomplishments
2. Projects completed or milestone achievements
3. Team recognition
4. Issues and challenges
5. Learning and improvements
6. Next week preview
7. Any announcements

**Outcomes**:
- Team celebrates progress
- Learning is captured
- Team aligns on next week

## Weekly Analysis (4 hours)

Beyond meetings, Sandy spends time on:

### 1. Performance Review (1 hour)

**Activities**:

1. **Review Weekly Metrics**
   - Strategic alignment progress
   - Project status
   - Quality performance
   - Team utilization
   - Schedule variance

2. **Identify Trends**
   - Are metrics improving?
   - Are new problems emerging?
   - Are risks materializing?
   - Are changes needed?

3. **Compare to Plan**
   - How is week's progress vs. plan?
   - Are we on track?
   - What adjustments are needed?

**Outcome**: Clear view of weekly performance and trajectory

### 2. Risk Assessment (30 minutes)

**Activities**:

1. **Review Identified Risks**
   - Are known risks stable, improving, or worsening?
   - Are mitigations working?
   - Do any risks need escalation?

2. **Identify Emerging Risks**
   - What new risks have emerged?
   - What is their probability and impact?
   - What mitigation is needed?

3. **Update Risk Register**
   - Record new risks
   - Update status of existing risks
   - Note mitigation actions

**Outcome**: Risk landscape is clear and managed

### 3. Priority Review (30 minutes)

**Activities**:

1. **Assess Current Priorities**
   - Are current priorities appropriate?
   - Has new information changed priorities?
   - Should priorities be adjusted?

2. **Plan Next Week**
   - What is the priority for next week?
   - What must happen?
   - What can wait?
   - What is blocked?

3. **Resource Allocation**
   - Are resources allocated appropriately?
   - Are there conflicts?
   - Do adjustments need to be made?

**Outcome**: Clear priorities for next week

### 4. Learning Capture (1 hour)

**Activities**:

1. **Gather Learning**
   - What was learned this week?
   - What went well?
   - What could be improved?
   - What patterns are emerging?

2. **Document Learning**
   - Record lessons learned
   - Note process improvements
   - Identify training needs
   - Update knowledge repository

3. **Identify Improvements**
   - What process improvements should be made?
   - What is quick to fix?
   - What requires planning?
   - What should be prioritized?

**Outcome**: Organizational learning is captured and improvements are planned

## Weekly Communication

**Team Updates**: Brief update on week's progress and next week's focus

**Stakeholder Updates**: Updates on their areas of responsibility, status, and risks

**Leadership Update**: Weekly summary for Managing Director covering:
- Week's progress vs. plan
- Key accomplishments
- Issues and risks
- Next week's focus
- Decisions needed

**Format**: Brief email or meeting depending on volume

## Weekly Routine Checklist

End of week, before closing:

- [ ] All meetings conducted
- [ ] Project status reviewed and updated
- [ ] Quality metrics reviewed
- [ ] Risk assessment completed
- [ ] Performance reviewed
- [ ] Learning captured and documented
- [ ] Next week's priorities are clear
- [ ] Escalations are prepared
- [ ] Team is recognized for accomplishments
- [ ] One-on-ones are scheduled
- [ ] Weekly communication is sent
- [ ] Action items are tracked

## Handling Weekly Disruptions

Sometimes weekly routine is disrupted:

### Crisis During Week

If crisis occurs:
- Weekly routine takes lower priority
- Focus shifts to crisis management
- At-risk meetings may be cancelled or shortened
- Catch-up happens after crisis is resolved

### High-Volume Week

If unexpectedly busy:
- Meetings still happen (discipline)
- Analysis might be shortened but still occurs
- Learning is still captured
- Next week is still planned

### Light Week

If unusually slow:
- Use extra time for deeper analysis
- Conduct more comprehensive reviews
- Plan ahead for busy periods
- Invest in improvements

## Weekly Routine Variations

Routine might vary:

### First Week of Month

Include planning for monthly activities:
- Schedule monthly business review
- Plan month-long initiatives
- Review monthly goals

### Last Week of Month

Include planning for monthly closing:
- Prepare monthly performance report
- Plan month-end review
- Prepare for next month

### Around Major Deadlines

Routine might be adjusted:
- More frequent check-ins
- Deeper risk monitoring
- Extra support for at-risk projects
- Shorter/streamlined other activities

## Weekly Reflection

Each week, Sandy reflects:

- **Effectiveness**: Was this week productive?
- **Pace**: Was pace sustainable?
- **Balance**: Was there balance between different responsibilities?
- **Issues**: What problems emerged?
- **Improvements**: What could be better?

This reflection builds continuous improvement in Sandy's own effectiveness.

## Building Weekly Discipline

Weekly routine becomes effective through:

- **Scheduled time**: Specific times blocked for routine
- **Meeting discipline**: Meetings happen as scheduled
- **Protected time**: Analysis time is protected
- **Consistency**: Routine is followed each week
- **Communication**: Team knows the rhythm

Over time, weekly routine becomes automatic and natural.
