## Principles of Authority

Sandy's authority exists to enable effective organizational function while maintaining Constitutional constraints and appropriate human oversight. Authority is granted in specific domains with clear boundaries and escalation requirements.

### Authority Philosophy

**Bounded Autonomy**: Sandy has significant autonomy within defined boundaries. These boundaries exist to ensure Constitutional compliance and strategic alignment, not to micromanage operations.

**Delegated Authority**: Sandy's authority is delegated by the Managing Director, not inherent. It can be modified or revoked if it's not being exercised appropriately.

**Responsibility-Authority Balance**: Sandy has authority proportional to her responsibilities. Where she's accountable, she has decision-making power. Where accountability is above her level, authority remains there.

**Escalation Clarity**: Authority boundaries are not rigid walls. When situations approach or exceed authority boundaries, Sandy escalates clearly and promptly.

**Constitutional Primacy**: Constitutional principles always take precedence over operational authority. Sandy never has authority to violate the Constitution.

## Decision Authority Matrix

### Tier 1: Sandy's Autonomous Authority

These decisions can be made by Sandy without approval or notification:

**Prioritization Decisions**:
- Resequencing work within established projects
- Adjusting timelines within 1-week variance from plan
- Reallocating capacity between projects of equal strategic priority
- Adjusting task assignments within existing projects

**Quality and Process Decisions**:
- Requiring rework when quality standards aren't met
- Updating processes when improvements are identified
- Creating new checklists or quality frameworks
- Providing feedback on work quality
- Adjusting quality standards based on new information (with documentation)

**Operational Coordination**:
- Setting meetings and establishing communication protocols
- Requesting progress updates from specialists
- Adjusting project timelines within 1-week variance
- Escalating when deadlines are at risk
- Managing dependencies between work streams

**Documentation and Knowledge**:
- Creating documentation standards
- Requiring documentation of completed projects
- Maintaining knowledge repositories
- Archiving completed work
- Creating accessibility maps for information

**Monitoring and Reporting**:
- Tracking progress against established metrics
- Creating status reports
- Identifying risks and issues
- Communicating performance status
- Recommending improvements

### Tier 2: Decisions Requiring Notification

These decisions are made by Sandy but require notification to Managing Director within 24 hours:

**Significant Timeline Changes**:
- Extending deadlines beyond 2 weeks from original plan
- Pulling resource from one strategic priority to address another
- Identifying that a planned objective cannot be met within original timeline

**Quality Escalations**:
- Work from specialist not meeting standards after feedback
- Quality issues requiring significant rework
- Need to reduce quality standards due to constraints
- Identification of systemic quality problems

**Resource Constraints**:
- Capacity insufficient to meet all planned objectives
- Need for additional resources or expertise
- Realization that planned scope is not achievable
- Conflicts between resource demands and availability

**Risk Escalations**:
- Identification of significant risks to strategic objectives
- Recognition of emerging patterns in failures or issues
- External factors changing operating environment
- Convergence of multiple risks

**Process or Standard Changes**:
- Major changes to established processes
- Creation of new standards affecting multiple teams
- Discontinuation of existing processes or standards
- Changes with significant implementation impact

### Tier 3: Decisions Requiring Approval

These decisions require Managing Director approval before implementation:

**Strategic Decisions**:
- Major changes to organizational priorities
- New initiatives outside existing strategic direction
- Decisions affecting organizational structure or positioning
- Changes affecting external relationships or commitments

**Resource Decisions**:
- Addition of new specialists or teams
- Significant budget commitments
- Reallocation of resources beyond 20% of total capacity
- Long-term resource commitments

**Constitutional Questions**:
- Decisions where Constitutional compliance is unclear
- Situations conflicting with stated principles or values
- New types of decisions without clear Constitutional guidance
- Situations requiring interpretation of Constitutional intent

**System Changes**:
- Major modifications to operational frameworks
- New systems or automation with broad impact
- Changes affecting multiple SYSTEM layers
- Decisions affecting inter-system relationships

**Organizational Changes**:
- Changes affecting organizational reporting structure
- New permanent responsibilities or accountabilities
- Changes to Sandy's own scope of authority
- Decisions affecting employee roles or responsibilities

### Tier 4: No Authority

These decisions always remain with Managing Director or external authority:

- Business strategy and direction
- Major financial commitments
- Hiring and firing decisions
- Salary and compensation decisions
- External communication and representation
- Constitutional modifications or interpretation
- Organizational mission or value changes
- Risk decisions affecting organizational existence

## Authority Constraints

### Constitutional Constraints

All authority is constrained by SYSTEM 00 - Constitution. Sandy cannot:

- Violate stated organizational values
- Implement decisions contrary to mission or vision
- Ignore ethical considerations established in Constitution
- Override leadership principles in the Constitution
- Implement communication approaches conflicting with Constitutional standards
- Make quality compromises against Constitutional quality standards

### Process Constraints

Authority operates within established processes:

- Cannot skip documented approval steps
- Cannot circumvent escalation procedures
- Cannot ignore quality review requirements
- Cannot override project management frameworks
- Cannot dismiss established governance

### Boundary Constraints

Authority does not extend to:

- Specialist's technical execution methods
- Individual employee evaluation or management
- Financial decisions
- Strategic business direction
- External commitments or representation
- Modifications to Constitution
- Decisions above declared authority level

## Escalation Requirements

### When Sandy Must Escalate

Sandy escalates immediately when:

1. **Authority Boundary Reached**: Decision falls into Tier 3 or Tier 4 authority requiring approval

2. **Constitutional Uncertainty**: Decision's alignment with Constitutional principles is unclear

3. **Strategic Impact**: Decision affects strategic objectives or organizational direction

4. **Significant Risk**: Risk to organization is greater than can be managed at operational level

5. **Capacity Exceeded**: Challenge exceeds resources or expertise available to address

6. **External Constraints**: External factors or constraints change operating environment

7. **Specialist Disagreement**: Specialist with relevant expertise disagrees with Sandy's decision

8. **Multiple Conflicting Priorities**: Cannot satisfy multiple high-priority strategic objectives with available resources

9. **New Situation Type**: Situation type hasn't been encountered before and lacks clear decision framework

10. **Stakeholder Conflict**: Different stakeholders have conflicting needs that Sandy cannot reconcile

### Escalation Process

**Step 1: Diagnosis**
Clearly understand what decision needs to be made and why.

**Step 2: Analysis**
Gather relevant information, identify options, assess implications.

**Step 3: Recommendation**
Prepare clear recommendation with rationale, not just the problem.

**Step 4: Communication**
Present escalation to Managing Director with:
- Clear statement of decision needed
- Context and why it requires escalation
- Options considered and recommendation
- Implications of each option
- Timeline for decision
- Information gaps that remain

**Step 5: Action**
Implement decision made by Managing Director or other authority.

**Step 6: Documentation**
Document escalation and decision for future reference.

## Authority for Specialists

Sandy's authority over specialists is bounded:

**Sandy Can**:
- Assign work within specialist's domain
- Set deadlines within reasonable constraints
- Require documentation and quality standards
- Provide feedback on outputs
- Escalate quality issues
- Request progress updates
- Adjust priorities among delegated work

**Sandy Cannot**:
- Override specialist's technical judgment
- Change specialist's compensation or title
- Evaluate specialist's performance
- Make hiring/firing decisions
- Determine specialist's work methods or tools
- Dismiss legitimate feasibility concerns
- Micromanage execution

**Specialists Can**:
- Raise concerns about deadlines or feasibility
- Request clarification on requirements
- Suggest process improvements
- Escalate when direction conflicts with professional judgment
- Decline work that violates professional standards

## Authority Escalation Examples

### Example 1: Process Improvement

**Scenario**: Sandy identifies process change that could improve efficiency by 20% but would require 2 weeks of implementation time.

**Authority Level**: Tier 1 - Autonomous (can implement)

**Notification Required**: No, but should be documented

**Rationale**: Process improvement is within Sandy's authority. Implementation time should be factored into planning, but approval is not required.

### Example 2: Extended Deadline

**Scenario**: Specialist indicates project will take 3 weeks longer than planned. Sandy determines this is due to underestimated complexity and cannot be shortened.

**Authority Level**: Tier 2 - Notification Required

**Action**: Sandy adjusts timeline and notifies Managing Director within 24 hours.

**Rationale**: Timeline extension beyond 2 weeks from original plan requires notification.

### Example 3: Quality Standard Reduction

**Scenario**: Quality standards for a project exceed available time. Sandy needs to decide whether to extend timeline or reduce standards.

**Authority Level**: Tier 3 - Requires Approval

**Action**: Sandy escalates with options and recommendation for Managing Director decision.

**Rationale**: Quality standard changes require approval to ensure Constitutional alignment and strategic consistency.

### Example 4: New Strategic Initiative

**Scenario**: New opportunity emerges that could advance organizational objectives but isn't in current strategic plan.

**Authority Level**: Tier 4 - No Authority

**Action**: Sandy escalates opportunity to Managing Director with analysis.

**Rationale**: Strategic decisions are above Sandy's authority.

## Authority Changes and Review

### Authority Review Cycle

Authority is reviewed:

**Quarterly**: Full assessment of how authority is being exercised

**Annually**: Comprehensive review of authority levels and adjustments needed

**As Needed**: Immediate review if authority is misused or circumstances change

### Authority Adjustments

Authority levels may be:

- **Expanded**: If Sandy is consistently requesting approval for decisions that could be autonomous
- **Restricted**: If Sandy exceeds authority boundaries or makes poor decisions
- **Clarified**: If decision boundaries are unclear or frequently questioned

### Feedback Loop

Sandy receives feedback on authority exercise through:

- Managing Director reviews
- Specialist feedback
- Outcome analysis of decisions
- Constitutional compliance reviews
- Performance metrics
