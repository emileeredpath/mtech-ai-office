## Knowledge Capture Philosophy

Knowledge capture is systematic effort to preserve learning before it's lost. If something is not captured, it stays in people's heads and leaves when they do. Capturing knowledge requires discipline and systems.

**Core Principle**: When important learning happens, it should be captured immediately or soon after, while fresh. Delay means loss of detail and context.

## Capture Triggers

### When to Capture

**Capture knowledge when**:

1. **Process Created or Refined**
   - New process designed
   - Process improved
   - Better way discovered
   - Significant change made

2. **Decision Made**
   - Important decision
   - Significant trade-off made
   - Rationale for decision
   - Context and constraints

3. **Problem Solved**
   - Unique problem encountered
   - Novel solution developed
   - Solution might help others
   - Learning captured

4. **Lesson Learned**
   - Success achieved
   - Failure analyzed
   - Mistake made and learned from
   - Insight gained

5. **Expertise Developed**
   - Deep expertise gained
   - Skills mastered
   - Domain knowledge acquired
   - Unique capability developed

6. **Best Practice Identified**
   - Efficient approach found
   - Quality improvement discovered
   - Cost savings identified
   - Time-saving method

7. **Customer/Market Insight**
   - Customer need identified
   - Market trend observed
   - Competitive intelligence
   - Customer feedback pattern

8. **Risk or Challenge Overcome**
   - Risk materialized and managed
   - Challenge overcome
   - Resilience demonstrated
   - Crisis handled

## Capture Methods

### Observation and Inference

**Capture through**:
- Watch expert at work
- Observe process execution
- Infer best practices
- Identify patterns
- Document observations

**Process**:
1. Observer watches work
2. Notes patterns and approach
3. Infers underlying knowledge
4. Documents learning
5. Validates with expert

### Conversation and Interview

**Capture through**:
- Structured conversation
- Knowledge interview
- Discussion with expert
- Q&A process
- Dialogue capture

**Process**:
1. Schedule time with expert
2. Ask probing questions
3. Listen and capture
4. Clarify and verify
5. Document conversation

**Good Questions**:
- "How do you approach this?"
- "What are the key decisions?"
- "What would you tell a new person?"
- "What's often missed?"
- "What surprised you?"

### Documentation

**Capture through**:
- Write process documentation
- Record procedures
- Capture frameworks
- Document checklists
- Create guides

**Documentation Types**:
- Process guides
- Decision frameworks
- Checklists
- FAQ documents
- Best practice guides
- Lessons learned documents

### Recording and Transcription

**Capture through**:
- Video recording
- Audio recording
- Meeting transcription
- Screen recording
- Archive for later review

**Use Cases**:
- Expert demonstration
- Important meeting
- Complex training
- Difficult conversation
- Historical record

### Note-Taking

**Capture through**:
- Real-time note-taking
- Meeting notes
- Project notes
- Learning notes
- Observations

**Discipline**:
- Capture during work
- Review and organize after
- Share with team
- Archive for reference

### After-Action Review

**Capture through**:
- Team debrief
- Structured reflection
- What happened? Why? What's next?
- Lessons identified
- Improvements noted

**Process**:
1. Complete project or event
2. Team gathers for debrief
3. Discuss what happened
4. Identify lessons
5. Document learning
6. Identify improvements

## Structured Knowledge Capture

### Template-Based Capture

**Use templates to standardize**:
- Process documentation
- Decision records
- Lessons learned
- Best practices
- Meeting decisions

**Templates include**:
- Title and purpose
- Context and background
- Key information
- Who, what, when, where
- Lessons and learning
- Next steps

### Process Documentation Template

**Key elements**:
- Process name and purpose
- Who performs the process
- When the process occurs
- Step-by-step procedures
- Key decision points
- Resources needed
- Common challenges
- Continuous improvement notes

### Decision Record Template

**Key elements**:
- What decision was made
- Why (rationale)
- Alternatives considered
- Key trade-offs
- Who decided
- When decided
- Impact and outcomes
- How it worked out

### Lessons Learned Template

**Key elements**:
- What happened
- What was the lesson
- Why is it important
- How will we apply it
- What will we do differently
- Who should know
- Impact of the lesson

## Capture Quality

### Clarity

**Captured knowledge is**:
- Clear and understandable
- Well-organized
- Complete
- Contextualized
- Accessible to readers

### Accuracy

**Captured knowledge is**:
- Correct
- Up-to-date
- Verified
- Validated by expert
- Free from errors

### Completeness

**Captured knowledge includes**:
- Necessary context
- Step-by-step details
- Rationale
- Examples
- Edge cases

### Accessibility

**Captured knowledge is**:
- Easy to find
- In appropriate format
- Available to those who need it
- Searchable
- User-friendly

## Timeliness of Capture

### Immediate Capture

**Capture immediately when**:
- Expert doing work
- Important moment
- Rich context
- Ready to share

**Benefits**: Rich detail, accurate, contextual

**Challenge**: Interrupts work

### Short-Term Capture

**Capture shortly after when**:
- Project complete
- Significant event
- Learning fresh
- Time to reflect

**Benefits**: Still detailed, allows reflection

**Practical**: Most common

### Retrospective Capture

**Capture later when**:
- Historical knowledge
- Institutional knowledge
- Patterns over time
- Research/interview needed

**Benefits**: Can synthesize multiple perspectives

**Challenge**: Detail may be lost

## Knowledge Capture Plan

**For important knowledge areas**:

1. **Identify**: What knowledge is critical?
2. **Source**: Who has this knowledge?
3. **Method**: How will we capture?
4. **Timeline**: When will we capture?
5. **Format**: How will we document?
6. **Validation**: Who verifies accuracy?
7. **Storage**: Where will we store?
8. **Access**: Who can access?
9. **Maintenance**: Who maintains?
10. **Review**: When do we review?

## Capture and Retention

**Systematic capture**:
- Prevents knowledge loss
- Preserves capability
- Enables transfer
- Supports growth
- Maintains history

**When person leaves**:
- Knowledge already captured
- Transfer already happened
- Others can continue work
- Continuity maintained
